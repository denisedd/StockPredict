    100, 75, 50 Years Ago

    MAY 31, 2012

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    1912 Japan Joins China Financing

    PEKING — Japan’s decision to participate in the financing of China upon the terms offered by the Four Power groups, and regardless of what action Russia may take, has been communicated to the interested Powers. Japan’s decision to co-operate with the United States, France, England and Germany is regarded here as an epochal development in Far Eastern politics which is fraught with momentous significance. When the invitation to participate in the loan was first extended by the four Powers, Japan replied that her acceptance would have to be conditional upon Russia’s acceptance. Japan’s present action means, therefore, open defection from Russia.

    1937 Five Die in Chicago Strikes

    CHICAGO — Murder indictments against members of the Chicago City police force will be demanded by the Steel Workers’ Organizing Committee as the result of last night’s riots in front of the Republic Steel Corporation plants in which five men were killed and eighty wounded, it was announced here today [May 31]. Trouble began when 1,500 strikers, marching four abreast, approached the gates of Republic Steel’s plant here. The company is the only one of three major steel corporations against which strikes are being conducted that has attempted to continue production. As the strikers came within one block of the gates, they apparently trespassed on a narrow strip of the company’s land, where Captain James Kilroy, of the Chicago police, was waiting with 150 officers. In the twenty-minute free-for-all that ensued, the police fired directly into the mob.

    1962 Eichmann Is Hanged by Israel

    TEL AVIV — Adolf Eichmann died on the gallows tonight [May 31] with the words “Long live Germany” on his lips. The former Gestapo colonel was executed for his leading role in the Nazi crimes against the Jews during World War II. He was pronounced dead at two minutes before midnight in Ramleh Prison, half-way between Tel Aviv and Jerusalem. Eichmann lost his final appeal for clemency earlier tonight when President Itzhak Ben-Zvi decided not to intervene in the conviction handed down by a special court and supported by the Supreme Court of Israel. Eichmann insisted that he had only carried out orders.
