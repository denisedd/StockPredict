    Get Back To Cool

    Welcome to the New Street Style App From Japan

    We all know there is a symbiotic relationship between fashion and social media. Fashion is an industry continually redefined by trends — inspiration is the oxygen, while creativity is the heartbeat. That inspiration can be found on social media’s open forums, where bold, creative people show off what they’re wearing and how they're wearing it, prompting rivals, fans and imitators to respond in kind. Check your Instagram, Facebook feed and favorite fashion blog: The results are a visual feast of originality.

    In Japan — a country known for avant-garde designers, progressive street style and a seemingly nationwide talent for subverting and initiating trends — there’s a popular fashion app filled with enough content to satisfy a style-inspiration binge. This app has the captivating here’s-what-the-cool-kids-are-wearing feel of a designer lookbook, as well as the friendly vibes of a family photo album. And the best news? It just arrived in the U.S.

    Meet the Blogger:
    Lindsay Marcella

    When Pittsburgh native and current New York City resident Lindsay Marcella started blogging, it was to promote her work as an interior designer. She had a good eye for home decor, but her followers were often curious about her makeup and clothing picks. “I was getting a lot of questions on Instagram, like, what lipstick are you wearing?, where’s your shirt from?, or can you blog about your workout routines?”

    Meet the Blogger:
    Lindsay Marcella

    At first she responded hoping the information she shared would help her increase the customer base of her online home goods store, but in fall 2015, she decided to create two distinct online destinations. Marcella’s lifestyle site documents her fashion, beauty and travel adventures with visuals that are sleek and appealing without being too precious. “I think the women that follow me say, I shop at those stores, that’s within my price range and she’s not wearing heels every day. She wears flats and it works,” says Marcella. “I think it’s relatable and unassuming.”

    Meet the Blogger:
    Denny Balmaceda

    When 28-year-old Denny Balmaceda first started keeping an online clothing diary, it was to help pass the time during his days right after high school working as a travel agent. “There was nothing to do on my lunch break,” says Balmaceda. “So my girlfriend then gave me a digital camera for Christmas and I was just like, why don’t I make a journal out of my outfits?” He eventually started posting to a few different social media platforms and launched a blog in 2011. “I would mimic high-fashion and super expensive clothes by just going to thrift stores,” he says. “They are all inspired by vintage pieces anyway.”

    Meet the Blogger:
    Denny Balmaceda

    His current fan base enjoys his retro-inspired aesthetic, which includes wide brim hats, high-waisted trousers, souvenir jackets and the occasional old school car in the background. Check out #dennyvintagewhips for a lesson in matching an outfit with an automobile. Balmaceda’s photo compositions are original, but he’s not trying to splinter his audience’s attention. He is in fact, a bit of a fashion photo purist. “I like you to focus on the outfit.”

    It’s called Wear (now available for iOS and Android) and, every day, members of the app’s community of 7 million active users upload more than 7,000 images of themselves dressed according to a daily hashtag (#widelegpants or #whitedenim, for example). They also tag the photos to show the designers represented in their outifts.

    Yusaku Maezawa, a Tokyo-based billionaire who recently added a $57 million painting by Jean-Michel Basquiat to his already formidable collection of contemporary art, launched the app about three years ago. Maezawa got his start running a modest mail-order business in the 1990s that eventually became the e-commerce giant Zozotown.

    When Maezawa decided to expand into fashion, he hoped to create an app that’s a democratic setting for users to conduct personal style experiments and share them with a community of like-minded innovators. “It’s an expression of the poster’s individuality through the designers and brands they choose and how their style is assembled,” says Masahiro Ito, CEO of engineering for Start Today, the parent company of Wear and Zozotown. “It speaks to who they are, while at the same time creating something that resonates with others. Fashion always has an aspirational aspect to it, and Wear can be a great source for finding new inspiration.”

    The “Back to Cool” theme for this month’s set of T Brand Studio hashtags invites users to curate outfits that work for late summer into fall, when days are still warm but evening temperatures can dip. Blogger Lindsay Marcella (read more about her in the Wear It’s At profile) says her strategy for transitional season dressing involves mixing cool-off pieces like blouses with cutout-back blouses and strappy sandals with cool-weather elements like denim and chunky sweaters. Fellow blogger Denny Balmaceda, also profiled, relies on jackets and a collection of 25 hats to layer up and down.

    One last to note to help keep the chic photos coming: Uploads from Wearers (yes, that’s what they’re called) that receive strong community feedback may also end up right here on this page. So start posting.
