    By Katherine Schulten
    January 28, 2016 3:46 pm January 28, 2016 3:46 pm

    Back in the day, it was twenty-fifteen,
    Gay marriage was legalized, and China wasn’t clean
    An earthquake in Nepal, 9000 dead,
    Let’s build a big wall, that’s what Donald Trump said

    — Opening lines of a rap by ECE Sabot, one of our 11 winners

    While our own year-end news quizzes spotlight the 50 or so stories we find most important each year, every December we also invite teenagers to write raps about the news that mattered most to them.

    This year, to no one’s surprise, one name dominated the posts. Whether rhyming about his candidacy, his plan to build a wall along the border of Mexico, his statements about Muslims, or just his hair, Donald Trump made it into almost every submission. Below, you can read what other news — from gay marriage and ISIS to Adele and “Star Wars” — inspired the winners.

    The hip-hop experts at Flocabulary, our annual partners for this contest, chose 11 favorites from 700 submissions. We’ve reproduced them here in no particular order, and exactly as originally written.
