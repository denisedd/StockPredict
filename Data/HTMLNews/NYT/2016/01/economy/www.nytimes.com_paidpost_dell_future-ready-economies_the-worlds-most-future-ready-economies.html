    What Cities Are Setting the Pace?

    Why are some cities better prepared for the future than others? The Dell Future Ready Economies Model seeks to find out by capturing the work of people, businesses and governments in today's most innovative cities.

    Human Capital Measures the human aspects of future readiness, such as whether a community has the right people with the right skills to drive meaningful innovation.

    Commerce Measures the business and political climate of local economies, including economic growth forecasts, productivity and instances of innovative programs such as incubators and public-private partnerships.

    Infrastructure Measures the underlying support systems and technologies that help people, businesses and governments innovate and create economies primed for growth and transformation.

    Explore Future-Ready Cities

    In every region of the United States, cities are transforming themselves to prepare for the future. By fostering collaboration, rethinking government and leveraging technology, they’re attracting the people — and businesses — to fuel their growth into the 21st century and beyond.

    The Dell Future Ready Economies Model analyzes more than 40 sets of data — covering human capital, commerce and infrastructure — to uncover what the most innovative cities are doing right, and what others can do, to make themselves future ready.
