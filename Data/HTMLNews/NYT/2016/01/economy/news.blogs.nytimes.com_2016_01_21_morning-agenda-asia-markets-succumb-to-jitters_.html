    ASIA MARKETS SUCCUMB TO JITTERS | Markets in Asia sank on Thursday, as traders surrendered to the fears that had sent shares tumbling the previous day, Amie Tsang reports in DealBook.
    Investor concerns over growth in China and oil prices drove global markets lower on Wednesday, pushing London and Japan into a bear market.

    Asian markets seemed to reflect few of these worries on Thursday morning, but the calm did not last past lunchtime. The Nikkei 225-share index, which had closed down 3.7 percent on Wednesday, lost 2.4 percent Thursday. The Hang Seng index in Hong Kong reversed the 1.5 percent it had gained in the morning to close 1.8 percent lower.

    Markets in mainland China were nervous throughout the day. They opened lower and the main Shanghai index was down 3.2 percent at the close, while Shenzhen shares finished down 4 percent.
