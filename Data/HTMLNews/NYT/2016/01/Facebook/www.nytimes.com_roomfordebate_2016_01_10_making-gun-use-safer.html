    Making Gun Use Safer

    Introduction

    Hannibal Hanschke/Reuters

    “If we can set it up so you can’t unlock your phone unless you’ve got the right fingerprint, why can’t we do the same thing for our guns?” President Obama said when he announced executive action on gun control last week. “If a child can’t open a bottle of aspirin, we should make sure that they can’t pull a trigger on a gun.” And while “issues like licensing, registration,” he said in a town hall meeting about guns on Thursday, “do not have “enough national consensus at this stage to even consider,’’ other measures similar to those used to ensure car safety have been proposed for guns.

    What sorts of measures could improve the safety, if not reduce the availability of guns?

    Debaters

    • Treat Gun Violence as a Public Health Issue

      Claire McCarthy, Boston Children's Hospital

      I talk to my patients' parents about gun safety. But there's so much more beyond my control that could be done.

    • Federal Micromanagement Won’t Work

      Ben Peterson, graduate student, Pepperdine

      The liberty to “keep and bear arms,” is more central to American liberty than the right to car ownership.

    • Regulate Guns the Way Cars Are

      Jamie Lincoln Kitman, Automobile Magazine

      The automobile and gasoline lobbies dwarf the power and financial influence of the N.R.A. And yet cars and their fuel have been regulated.

    • Smart Guns Are a Delusion

      Charles C. W. Cooke, National Review

      It's wrong to compare guns to other products. Guns are killing machines designed to work as effectively and reliably as possible.

    • Smart Guns Are Smart Policy

      Stephen Teret, Johns Hopkins University

      A study of accidental shootings found that 37 percent could have been prevented if the guns involved had identification technology.

    • Open Carry Laws Could Increase Gun Violence

      Judson Robinson III, Houston Area Urban League

      Police won't know who the bad guys are and in high-crime neighborhoods fears could lead to confrontations.
