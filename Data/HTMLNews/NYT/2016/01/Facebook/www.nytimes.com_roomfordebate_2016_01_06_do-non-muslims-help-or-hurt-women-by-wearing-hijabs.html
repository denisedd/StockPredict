    Do Non-Muslims Help or Hurt Women by Wearing Hijabs?

    Introduction

    Larycia Hawkins, a professor at Wheaton College, an evangelical Christian institution, wore a headscarf in solidarity with Muslim women but was put on leave for saying Islam and Christianity worship the same god. Charles Rex Arbogast/Associated Press

    In the United States, bigots have attacked Muslim women for using a headscarf, sometimes called the hijab, that many feel Islam requires them to wear. In Muslim countries women have been attacked for not wearing it. On Feb. 1, World Hijab Day, women of all persuasions will wear headscarves in solidarity against Islamophobes. But some say the headscarf is not a tenet of Islam but a symbol of oppression created by conservative clerics to set women apart. Does a bare head show greater respect for Muslim women?

    Debaters

    • Good Intentions Perpetuate Inequity

      Asra Q. Nomani, Muslim Reform Movement and Hala Arafa, retired journalist

      Well-intentioned women perpetuate a lethal global war of ideas sexually objectifying women and absolving men of personal responsibility.

    • Not the Only Way to Show Solidarity, but the Most Visible One

      Dilshad D. Ali, Patheos

      As anti-Muslim backlash surges in the Republican campaign, those most likely to be on the receiving end wear hijabs.

    • A Matter of Feminism, Aesthetics and Solidarity

      Celene Ibrahim, Tufts University

      The paradox is that whether a woman wears one or not fault finders point fingers and pass judgement on her intentions and preferences.

    • In My Life, Headscarves Have Been Symbols of Oppression

      Nushin Arbabzadah, U.C.L.A.

      For Kabul's communist government and the Western-backed mujahedin, my hair was a symbol to be negotiated without my consent.

    • More Than Symbolic Solidarity Is Needed

      Shenila Khoja-Moolji, Columbia University’s Teachers College

      Solidarity can begin with donning a hijab, but it must evolve to address the causes of racism, Islamophobia and the oppression of women.
