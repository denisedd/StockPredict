    Send Us the Political Ads You See on Facebook

    By THE NEW YORK TIMES APRIL 14, 2016

    This project has ended. Thank you for participating.

    As this election season enters its frenzied final months, candidates up and down the ticket are preparing to spend vast sums on advertising — on television, of course, but also increasingly online.

    More than ever, political campaigns are using companies like Facebook to target voters with specific advertisements based on their interests, demographic characteristics, online behavior and other personal information. That means two people living on the same street in Ohio are may see entirely different ads online, even if they see the same ones when they turn on the T.V.

    We'd like to learn more about how campaigns are using these technologies to target you.

    We've built a tool that allows you to share the ads you see on Facebook with our reporters and editors. You can install it in your web browser using the instructions below.

    Once you do, you will see a small button appear above each ad and sponsored post in your Facebook feed, like this:

    Simply click that button whenever you see a political ad to send an image of that ad to us. We will use that information to inform our reporting into political advertising.

    Optionally, you can also provide us with your email address. If you do, we might reach out with questions about the ads you submitted in an effort to better understand why you might have seen them.

    This project has ended. Thank you for participating. Here's how to uninstall.

    The tool is installed as a browser extension and is compatible with the Google Chrome and Mozilla Firefox web browsers. It does not work with Internet Explorer, Safari or other browser types.

    We have detected that you are using an incompatible browser. Please open this page in Chrome or Firefox.

    Google Chrome

    * Type chrome://extensions into your address bar.

    * Find "The New York Times Political Ad Tracker" in the list

    * Click the trash can on the right.

    * Click "Yes" on the popup.

    Mozilla Firefox

    * Type about:addons into your URL bar

    * Click the “Extensions” link at the left

    * Find "The New York Times Political Ad Tracker" in the list

    * Click “Remove”.

    Frequently Asked Questions
    (and Privacy Policy)

    What information do you collect?

    When you send us an ad, we will receive: an image of the ad as it appears on your screen; the underlying text of the ad (if any); the reason that Facebook says you’re seeing that ad (if available); your email address, if you provided it to us; the version of your web browser; and the position of the ad on the page, which we use for debugging purposes.

    What will you use this information for?

    We will use the ad images to inform our reporting into digital political advertising. If you give it to us, we might also use your email address to contact you with questions. We might publish images or descriptions of the ads, but they will not contain identifying information unless we obtain your permission.

    Will you publish what I submit?

    As part of our reporting, we may publish some of the ad information that you submit. We will also publish aggregate information -- for example, the number of variations of a particular ad that were submitted by our readers. Anything that could identify you will be removed.

    We will not publish your name or email address in connection with your participation in this project (unless you give us your permission, for example, if we wanted to interview you).

    We will not publish the data as a whole, but portions of the data, with personally identifiable information removed, may be shared with academic researchers.

    Why only Facebook ads?

    Facebook advertising is an especially popular and powerful tool for campaigns, with some of the most sophisticated ad targeting capabilities on the market. That makes it a good place to start. We are considering including other types of online advertising in the future.

    Does it work on mobile devices?

    Not yet. These browser extensions only work on desktop and laptop computers. They also cannot be installed in Facebook's native mobile applications.

    Does the tool work on Mac computers?

    Yes, as long as you use Google Chrome or Mozilla Firefox (not Safari) to browse the web.

    Does it work on Windows/P.C. computers?

    Yes, as long as you use Google Chrome or Mozilla Firefox (not Internet Explorer or Edge) to browse the web.

    What if I am not sure if an ad is political?

    Feel free to submit it anyway and let us be the judge.
