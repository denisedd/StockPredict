    BIGGER IS BETTER FOR HEALTH CARE COMPANIES | The entire health care industry seems to be following the same playbook, Leslie Picker reports in DealBook. And the advice seems to be to team up with a similar company so that you can become the leading provider and use your clout to negotiate with hospitals and health insurers.

    More than $40 billion of deals were announced on Thursday alone.

    Abbott will buy St. Jude Medical, strengthening its position in the market for cardiovascular devices. Sanofi made an offer for Medivation, which makes drugs for prostate cancer, and AbbVie got its hands on Stemcentrx, an eight-year-old biotechnology company focused on novel cancer treatments.

    Regulation, new methods of payment and record consolidation mean health care companies see little choice but to scale up.

    The Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services have already mandated that payments for different requirements, be they hospital stays or drugs, are bundled together for people getting knee and hip replacements. Similar bundles of payment are expected for cancer and cardiovascular disease as part of a push to make health care more efficient.

    Companies are trying to position themselves to be the provider of choice for these bundled deals, but investors did not appear to reward these expensive moves on Thursday.
