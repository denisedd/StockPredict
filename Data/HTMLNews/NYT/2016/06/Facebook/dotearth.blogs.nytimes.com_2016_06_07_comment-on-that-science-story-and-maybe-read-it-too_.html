    Comment on That Shared Science Story – and Maybe Read it, Too!

    By Andrew C. Revkin
    June 7, 2016 6:17 am June 7, 2016 6:17 am

    I keep trying to find ways to “make veracity cool.”

    One effort of mine came several years ago, in writing the introduction to Darryl Cunningham’s clever graphic nonfiction book, “How to Fake a Moon Landing — Exposing the Myths of Science Denial” (nice review). You can read my introduction at the end of this post.

    Then along comes a study like the one featured a couple of days ago on The Science Report: “Study: 70% of Facebook users only read the headline of science stories before commenting.” Click it and weep, or laugh.

    While the Science Report item is humor, I’m sure you detect more than a hint of truth in it. Things are far worse on Twitter, as far as I can tell from various analytics. Although marketers, at least, are working hard to get you to engage.

    Even so, I’m still convinced that a key to social and environmental progress lies in testing new ways to share insights and observations and shape ideas using online and mobile media. It won’t happen without work, and without intentionality. Pope Francis made this point beautifully in a passage buried deep in the encyclical. Here’s that “hidden gem”:

    Below you’ll find the introduction I wrote for Cunningham’s book, which was published in the United States in 2013 (along with a teacher’s guide). I’m just sorry it took so long to post this here!
