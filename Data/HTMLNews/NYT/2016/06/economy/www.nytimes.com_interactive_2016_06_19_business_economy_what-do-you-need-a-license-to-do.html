    Quiz: Barriers to Work

    By KEVIN GRANVILLE JUNE 18, 2016

    State licensing rules foster a bizarre maze of obstacles for employment. Nearly 30 percent of the American work force requires a license to work, up from about 10 percent in the 1970s. Getting certifications can require months or years of expensive education and testing. Economists on the left and right agree that excessive licensing rules harm economic growth but the standards are fiercely defended by the groups that benefit from them. Test what you know about licensing in the country.

    1 of 5
    The District of Columbia requires people doing this job to be certified by passing an examination (which charges fees of about $1,300) and accumulating years of experience and education. Neighboring states of Maryland and Virginia have no such requirements. What’s the job?
    Carpenter
    Interior designer
    Court clerk
    Fire sprinkler system tester
    Travel agent
    2 of 5
    Only one state in the country requires forest workers to get a license. Which state?
    Maine (which is 89 percent covered by forest)
    Georgia (67 percent)
    North Carolina (59 percent)
    Connecticut (55 percent)
    Hawaii (42 percent)
    3 of 5
    After Hurricane Katrina, the monks of St. Joseph Abbey in southern Louisiana raised money by selling a product that they had made for themselves for years. Before long a state professional board told them to stop because they were not licensed to sell this product. The monks sued, and won in federal court in 2011. After the board appealed, a three-judge panel affirmed the lower court ruling, saying that “mere economic protection of a particular industry” was not a “legitimate government purpose.” What product were the monks selling?
    Coffins
    Hooded robes
    Brandy
    Hot sauce
    Nut butters
    4 of 5
    Illinois is one of several states that can deny an applicant for a barber’s license because of a previous misdemeanor. Applicants for occupational licenses in all states risk being permanently barred from a variety of occupations because they have an arrest or conviction record. Critics point to cases when the conviction occurred decades earlier or had little connection to the position being sought. What proportion of adult Americans have a criminal record?
    1 in 7
    1 in 5
    1 in 3
    5 of 5
    Just as states vary in the occupations they choose to require a license, some states have a far higher percentage of licensed workers than others. A recent study found that South Carolina’s work force has the lowest percentage of workers who are licensed or certified, at 12 percent. Which state has the highest percentage?
    Texas
    California
    Massachusetts
    Iowa
    Wisconsin
