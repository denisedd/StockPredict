    Jessica Silver-Greenberg and Michael Corkery report in DealBook.

    Uber and Lyft make their drivers sign an arbitration clause. Square, the mobile payment processor, requires that employees agree to bring disputes to arbitration.

    WeWork, a $16 billion start-up that rents office space, fired Tara Zoumer, an associate community manager, when she refused to sign a policy that could require employees to ultimately resolve disputes through arbitration instead of in the courts.

    Arbitration clauses can seem to conflict with the professed goals that start-ups often have of upending business as usual and being open with employees.
