    Credit card companies, banks and other lenders have used the fine print of their contracts to keep consumers out of court and push them into arbitration where they are forced to battle powerful companies alone.

    The new rules would not allow them to do that. The changes would not apply to existing accounts, but consumers would be free to pay off their old loans and open new accounts.
