    A FUND WITH VIRTUAL CAPITAL, BUT NO CAPITALIST | A venture capital fund that calls itself the Decentralized Autonomous Organization has come out of nowhere in the last month to attract about $152 million from investors worldwide, making it the most successful crowdfunded venture ever,
    Nathaniel Popper reports in DealBook.

    The cryptocurrency start-up may not even be legal and will face regulatory obstacles, but it might well upend virtual investing. Thousands of mostly anonymous investors have heard about it through word of mouth and sent money, in the form of Ether, a freshly coded form of virtual currency. These investors are doing the digital equivalent of buying into a bakery with no baker, no menu and no assurance that the ovens will even be delivered.

    After it collects Ether from investors, the D.A.O. aims to put the money into other digital currency start-ups. The investing decisions will be made through online polling of shareholders.

    Although virtual currencies are rapidly being embraced by mainstream Wall Street firms and central banks, the D.A.O. returns to the more radical ambitions of virtual currencies. It is set up according to computer code. There are no human executives.

    The basic code was written by a 32-year-old German programmer, Christoph Jentzsch. But he is not set to have any continuing role and the D.A.O. does not hold the money of investors. Instead, investors own D.A.O. tokens that give them rights to vote on potential projects. Mr. Jentzsch said on Wednesday in an interview that he thought the structure absolved him of any legal responsibility for what could happen with the project.
