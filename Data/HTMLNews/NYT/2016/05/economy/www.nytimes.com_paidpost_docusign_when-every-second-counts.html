    How Digital Transactions Are Enabling a Truly Frictionless Economy

    In today’s digital economy, seconds can mean the difference between a happy customer and a lost sale. Companies invest billions of dollars improving IT systems and reworking business processes to gain crucial seconds and remove other barriers that impede a deal’s closing. Yet many overlook a major source of efficiency-sapping friction: paper documents.

    In today’s digital economy, seconds can mean the difference between a happy customer and a lost sale. Companies invest billions of dollars improving IT systems and reworking business processes to gain crucial seconds and remove other barriers that impede a deal’s closing. Yet many overlook a major source of efficiency-sapping friction: paper documents.

    The Paper Trail

    Paper-based transactions, such as contracts and mortgages, that require signatures and multiple approvals are handed, faxed or overnighted from company to customer and back again, where they are routed through multiple departments.

    TheDigital HighwayDigital Highway

    Digital transaction management systems guide customers and companies through automated, pre-defined processes, where signatures and approvals are captured securely and electronically, saving time and money while delighting customers in the process.

    The faster pace of life and business in the digital age sets expectations among customers and business partners. Research shows people visit a website less often if it is slower than a competitor’s by just 250 milliseconds — or a quarter of a second. Whether a customer is seeking information or completing a transaction, efficiency matters. In fact, one study finds that 35 percent of consumers stopped doing business with companies that provide poor service.

    As other sources of friction are removed, hand-signed documents that must be faxed, scanned and emailed, or returned by mail have emerged as a major culprit costing organizations precious time and, in turn, customers and revenue. The U.S. government alone reports a total of 11.4 billion hours are spent each year collecting information from about 10,000 official forms. In addition, governments and businesses face the cost in time and money to store, track and redo records if they’re lost in the shuffle.

    Quite simply, paper is no longer the way to do business when every second counts.

    Need for Speed

    Speed is a major benefit of moving from paper-based to digital transactions. Paper documents requiring ink signatures must be mailed back and forth — taking days, weeks or even months longer to complete than digital processes accomplishing the same task in seconds.

    Paving without Paper

    The Nevada Department of Transportation (NDOT) has gone digital for the execution of contracts related to road construction and repair projects. Teresa Schlaffer, a business process analyst at NDOT, says that contracts executed digitally get completed weeks faster than those approved using paper. After conducting a pilot in which NDOT tested five digital contracting processes, the organization is now moving documents through 200+ digital processes — each of which may require up to 60 different approvals and digital signatures. As a result, the department has reduced the number of days required to execute a road-repair contract from 28 to just four.

    • Approvals in a Hurry

      In September 2014, a flash flood washed out almost 50 miles of Interstate 15, a major artery linking Nevada with Utah. The road had to be reconstructed as quickly as possible to get cars and trucks through. Fortunately, NDOT had already begun using paperless transactions for road repair contracts.

    • The Road to Repair

      Even in an emergency, a surety bond must be issued and the offices of the attorney general and the governor must sign off on Nevada road repair contracts. Using paper, the process can take weeks. Using digital processes, NDOT executed the I-15 repair contract in just 45 minutes.

    • Flash Fix

      A contractor completed enough of the work to get a lane open in each direction between Nevada and Utah in only five days — much less time than it would have taken just to get the work approved, when using paper documents.

    Security Baked In

    Digital transactions are not only more efficient than paper transactions, but they are also more secure. Digital signatures can be verified by multiple factors such as email, SMS and phone call confirmation, and are protected by bank-grade encryption.

    Secure Transactions for the NYSE

    The New York Stock Exchange is the world’s largest stock exchange, as measured by market capitalization of its listed issues. As a cornerstone of the world economy, speed and security matter both when executing trades and communicating that information to the world. The NYSE licenses data to 900 market data vendors and 270,000 professional subscribers. The licenses, which previously took up to a week to process using paper documents, are now granted in less than 24 hours using DocuSign. The digital process, which has built in bank-grade encryption and multi-factor authentication for digital signatures, passed stringent requirements for security and enforceability at the NYSE. "The feedback," says Peggy Sullivan, director for market data administration at NYSE, "has been outstanding."

    Access Anytime, Anywhere

    Paper documents are routinely lost in transition as they are moved from place to place and person to person, and sometimes never reach their intended destinations. In contrast, digital documents can be accessed anytime, anywhere, by any authorized individual.

    Accelerate Medical Research

    Dr. BJ Rimel, a gynecologic oncologist at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center in Los Angeles, had a problem. She and her colleagues couldn’t find enough participants for cancer research studies. That changed in 2013, when they did away with paper consent forms. Instead of having to fill out paperwork in the doctor’s office, interested participants could sign up online to be contacted about research opportunities. The result, says Rimel, is more applications and much greater participation in research that can save lives. “We were able to close some studies that had been open for years without being able to find the right people,” she says. “We were able to find participants very quickly online to complete our research faster and accelerate new treatments.”

    Easy to Use

    With paper, it’s not always clear what needs to be signed where, and by whom. With digital processes, users are guided where to initial and sign without missing a field. Documents are then automatically routed to the right people.

    Digital Transaction Rooms

    Fox & Roach Realtors, an affiliate of Berkshire Hathaway HomeServices, is transforming its business — from agents toting stacks of file folders around from customer to customer, to using digital signature software and virtual transaction rooms. These days, agents and all other parties to a real estate transaction — including the clients — can access a secure online portal to review, sign and send documents related to individual home purchases without having to handle paperwork. "This provides a truly ubiquitous solution for everyone involved," says BHHS Fox & Roach CIO Scott Crowley. As a result, DocuSign is providing a better overall customer experience throughout the home buying process.

    The efficiency adds up: Fannie Mae estimated that an electronic mortgage process could shorten the time it takes to close by 30 days and save about $1,100 per mortgage, or about $1 billion per year.

    Once you go through one signing experience, the buy-in is pretty much immediate. Everyone sees that it’s going to make their lives so much easier.

    — Teresa Schlaffer, Nevada Department of Transportation business process analyst.

    Join the Digital Document Revolution

    In an age when speed of access, service and execution make all the difference between success and failure for organizations of every size and stripe, and efficiency is being pursued at every level, digital transactions are emerging as the new standard for creating a truly frictionless economy. Transactions that require paper documents and ink signatures to be mailed back and forth, scanned or faxed simply can’t compete with more efficient and secure digital transactions.
