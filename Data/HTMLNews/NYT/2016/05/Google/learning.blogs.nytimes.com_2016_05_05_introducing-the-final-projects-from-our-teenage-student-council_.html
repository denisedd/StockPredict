    Introducing the Final Projects From Our Teenage Student Council

    By Katherine Schulten
    May 5, 2016 1:49 pm May 5, 2016 1:49 pm

    Update: Find all the projects here.

    Though we reinvent this blog in modest ways every summer, this school year we took on an experiment that frankly scared us a little.

    In September we created a Student Council of teenagers who would, via email and Google docs, advise us on content, and even write occasional pieces for the blog.

    Though we had never met any of them in person, all 22 members of the fall council had been winners of our Summer Reading Contest, so we knew they were good writers, with real interest in current events.

    That first semester, we invited the council to choose articles from The Times that would engage other people their age and, every week, share them with us — along with the reasons they chose them.

    Within days we could see that our experiment wasn’t going to be much of a risk at all: We were instantly both delighted by and dependent on the steady stream of thoughts they sent our way.

    We used their contributions to come up with our daily Student Opinion questions and News Q’s, but we also published two fuller collections of their work:

    Read This | Recommendations From the Teenagers on Our Student Council

    Home, Food, Family, Giving: Nonboring Thanksgiving Reading Chosen by Our Student Council

    In January we chose a new group — and this time we invited any Times-loving teenager in the world to apply.

    In response to feedback from the fall council, we also gave this new group of 25 more freedom. Though they continued to advise us on daily content, and created a collection of Times must-reads for Women’s History Month, we also invited them to design their own final projects, alone or in groups of up to four.

    The only rule we gave them is the same rule we abide by ourselves in everything we post: It had to somehow meet the mission of “teaching and learning with The New York Times.”

    Well, get ready, because from now through the end of the school year, you’ll be reading the wonderful things they came up with, all of which we’ll tag “Student Council” so you can find them.
