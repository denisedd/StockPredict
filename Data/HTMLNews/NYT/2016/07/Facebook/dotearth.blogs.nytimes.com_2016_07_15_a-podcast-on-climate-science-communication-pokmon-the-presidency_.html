    How did this quirky project come about?

    A relentless willingness to experiment is required if the goal is to stay connected with audiences inÂ a fast-changing media landscape. That’s particularly true with a “super wicked” (dare I say “boring“?) issue like global warming, or a wonky one like clean-energy policy.

    Arctic Meltdown

    Last week’s chat explored Eric’s question: “Is it time to freak out about the Arctic?”

    The answer is mixed. There are signs that big changes in Arctic ice and weather patterns might contribute disruptively to storm severity and the like in northern temperate zones.

    But Jacquelyn described evidence showing that Arctic ecosystems, having evolved in a changeable climate, look pretty resilient. I mentioned my relevant coverage of amazing “pop-up forests” in the far North.

    Jacquelyn also riffed on one of her favorite extinct (for now?) mammals — the woolly mammoth and laid out the ecological and climatic arguments for repopulating the tundra with these foragers.

    She and I agreed that, if anything, folks should be far more concerned about the tropics in a warming climate, given how many regions are close to physical limits for heat now and other factors, like fragmentation of rain forests and pollutionÂ impacts on reefs, are adding stress.

    Jacquelyn also described how she and some other science and climate bloggers found relevance in the “winter is coming” theme of “Game of Thrones.”
