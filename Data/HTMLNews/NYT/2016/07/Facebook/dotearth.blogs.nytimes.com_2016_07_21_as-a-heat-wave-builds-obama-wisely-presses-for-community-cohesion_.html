    That resilience officer job title has become formalized of late as a growing list of city governments have joined the 100 Resilient Cities project of the Rockefeller Foundation. A big part of the job is to dissect a city as both a physical and social system and foster collaborative networks that can speed the identification of, and solutions to, emerging risks. The resilience officers, themselves, also form a global network, sharing what they learn.

    What made Obama’s Twitter note particularly welcome was his last point: “Check on your neighbors.”

    It reflects the president’s understanding that resilience is only partly about infrastructure, agency funding and the like; it’s also about community cohesion. It’s vital to have the capacity to recognize threats (thanks to sustained investments, at least so far, in weather and climate monitoring and analysis and the like).
