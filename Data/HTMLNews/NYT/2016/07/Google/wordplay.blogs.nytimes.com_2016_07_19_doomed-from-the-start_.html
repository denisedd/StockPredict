    Doomed From the Start

    By Deb Amlen
    July 19, 2016 10:00 pm July 19, 2016 10:00 pm

    WEDNESDAY PUZZLE — When last we saw Gordon Johnson, he was making his New York Times Crossword debut in a scientific manner. Today, he’s back to remind us of the great romances that have developed as a result of starring in movies.

    I found this to be a relatively easy and smooth solve for a Wednesday, but that’s not a bad thing. You want a bit of pushback from your midweek puzzle, but not enough so that you give up. My pushback came in the form of the former FINNish currency, the markka.

    Today’s Theme

    Mr. Johnson’s theme consists of four pairs of movie stars who have gotten romantically involved with each other, and they are:

    Liz TAYLOR and Richard BURTON
    Lauren BACALL and Humphrey BOGART
    Angelina JOLIE and Brad PITT
    Annette BENING and Warren BEATTY

    At first, I was confused by the revealer, because JOLIE/PITT and BENING/BEATTY are notoriously successful couples, at least as far as their press agents are concerned. But I think that the revealer, STAR CROSSED LOVE, has less to do with the relative success of the couples and more to do with the fact that these pairs are stars who fell in love and are now crossed in the puzzle.

    Tricky Clues

    – 9A: It’s worth noting that you will see alternate spellings in crosswords for words you think you know. For example, I plunked in KABOB here, but today, it’s spelled KEBAB, another variant. To be fair, the KEBAB spelling appears to be much more common, with 49.5 million hits on Google versus 4.9 million hits for KABOB.

    – 27A: ENSOUL is an odd word that doesn’t come up a lot, but it’s a thing. If you’re not sure how you’re supposed to get a word like that, the answer is you’re not. You are supposed to get it through the crossings, say “Huh” to yourself, and assign it to the Now I Know portion of your brain.

    – 24A: Wordplay alert! “Get into the pool?” is just what many of us would like to do during this heat wave, but today we’re talking about placing a BET, as in getting into a football pool.

    – 3D: Hands up if you thought Abraham Lincoln. Me too. The answer to the clue “Lincoln’s locale,” however, is NEBRASKA.

    – 13D: Clever wordplay here, too. Your first thought might have been of treasure maps, but today the clue “Where “X” may mark the spot” refers to a BALLOT.

    – 28D: I’ve never had the pleasure of taking a dip in one, but the answer to “Spring that’s unusually warm?” is OASIS. I don’t know if the water in an OASIS is actually warm, but the weather around it is.
