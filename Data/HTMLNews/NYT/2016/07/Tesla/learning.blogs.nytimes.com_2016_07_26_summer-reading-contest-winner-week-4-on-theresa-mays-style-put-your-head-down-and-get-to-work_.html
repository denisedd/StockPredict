    Summer Reading Contest Winner, Week 4 | On ‘Theresa May’s Style: Put Your Head Down and Get to Work’

    By Katherine Schulten
    July 26, 2016 8:01 am July 26, 2016 8:01 am

    Week 4 of our Summer Reading Contest ran from July 8 to July 14, days that saw the United States reeling from the attacks on police officers in Dallas and the struggle to find common ground on issues of race, policing and gun violence.

    Many of the 779 students who entered took on these topics, and we honor some of them below as runners-up and honorable mentions. But we chose Catherine Zhang’s essay on another big news topic that week as our winner. Below, her thoughts on the new British prime minister, Theresa May, and “how she will lead a deeply divided Britain through the uncertainty that is Brexit.”

    May, who is a relative moderate in the Conservative Party and only the second woman to become the British prime minister, has appeared thus far to be a competent and pragmatic politician â Steven Erlanger and other reporters have compared the former home secretary to Angela Merkel more than Margaret Thatcher. I, for one, admire her initiative in helping more women get elected to Parliament and her reputation as a tough negotiator.

    However, her decision to appoint Boris Johnson, a staunch proponent of Brexit, as foreign minister has made me quite skeptical. The man is a strictly undiplomatic diplomat and has inevitably been compared to Trump (look him up, the resemblance is uncanny), but perhaps May has some underlying logic to this.

    A former colleague once described her as a “bloody difficult woman,” and I hope that is what Britain needs in this time of May-hem.
