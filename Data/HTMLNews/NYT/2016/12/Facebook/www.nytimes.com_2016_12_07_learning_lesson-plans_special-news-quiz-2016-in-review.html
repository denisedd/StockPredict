Special News Quiz | 2016 in Review

By Katherine Schulten

  • Dec. 7, 2016

Updated: Dec. 31, 2017, including updated handouts.

In a special section called Turning Points, The Times writes, “2016 will enter history as a pivot that revealed the depth of the fears, alienation and frustration of our times, abruptly upending many of our assumptions about the future.”

See what you remember about the events of this dramatic year by taking our special news quiz, which we’ll continue to update until the end of December.

You can fill in the blanks with your own guesses, or scroll down to find a scrambled list of words that were removed. Clicking on each blank will take you to the original Times article from which the information came.

We have also created this printable version of the quiz, as well as this one-page sheet that lists just the answer choices.

We also invite students to try their hands at writing rhymes about the news of 2016 via the Year in Rap contest we’re running with Flocabulary through Jan. 10.

And if you finish our quiz and feel as if you’re just getting warmed up, feel free to test yourself on the news of 2009, 2010, 2011, 2012, 2013, 2014 or 2015 — or see what you remember about the whole first decade of this millennium.

Our regular 10-question Weekly News Quiz will be back on Jan. 10.

_________

1. In January, an armed antigovernment group occupied an Oregon _________ for 40 days, calling for Washington to hand control of federal lands to ranchers and local governments and seeking to unwind a century of policy that has shaped the West. (The end of the standoff left one protester dead and 25 others indicted, but in a surprising verdict, the leaders were later acquitted of federal conspiracy and weapons charges.)

2. _________, the infinitely changeable, fiercely forward-looking songwriter who taught generations of musicians about the power of drama, images and personas, died on Jan. 10, two days after his 69th birthday.

3. President Obama signed an emergency declaration for _________, Mich., in January that cleared the way for federal aid for the city, which began struggling with a drinking water crisis after damaged pipes started leaching lead. Investigations later revealed that state officials had rejected reports of problems for months while providing bottled water to state workers, and that thousands of children were exposed. By the end of 2016, the crisis was still not resolved.

4. In the first contest of the presidential race, voters in Iowa gathered to caucus on Feb. 1. _________, powered by a surge of support from evangelical Christians, dealt a humbling loss to Donald J. Trump. The close vote between Hillary Clinton and _________ helped show, according to a Times analysis, that “in many ways, the focus of the 2016 campaign has turned to easing the palpable frustrations of a large portion of white working-class Americans who believe that the country no longer works for them.”

5. The World Health Organization declared the _________ virus and its suspected link to birth defects an international public health emergency in February, a rare move that signaled the seriousness of the outbreak.

6. A team of scientists announced in February that they had heard and recorded the sound of two black holes colliding a billion light-years away, a fleeting chirp that fulfilled the last prediction of _________’s general theory of relativity.

7. The 50th edition of the Super Bowl featured the Denver Broncos, led by _________ in the twilight of his famed career, against the Carolina Panthers, represented by their pioneering quarterback, Cam Newton. The Broncos won, but the halftime show, in which _________ performed a song with “potent declarations of black pride,” resulted in criticism from some.

8. Justice _________, whose transformative legal theories, vivid writing and outsize personality made him a leader of a conservative intellectual renaissance in his three decades on the Supreme Court, was found dead on Feb. 13. An epic Washington political battle then began to take shape as Republicans said they would refuse to act on any Supreme Court nomination by President Obama. They then kept this promise by refusing to take up the Supreme Court nomination of Judge _________, whom Mr. Obama nominated on March 16.

9. The ________ claimed responsibility for deadly bombings that terrorized Brussels on March 22, killing more than 30 people at the city’s main international airport and in a subway station at the heart of the city, near the headquarters of the European Union.

10. President Obama and President _________ of Cuba appeared together in Havana on March 21, the first such official contact between their two governments in more than a half-century of Cold War hostility.

11. In April, millions of leaked documents that came to be known as the _________ Papers revealed vast wealth hidden by politicians and powerful figures, spurred public outrage across the globe, and set off criminal investigations on at least two continents.

12. Legislators in _________ voted on April 17 to approve impeachment of Dilma Rousseff, the nation’s first female president. She was impeached in August, and removed from office, the capstone of a power struggle that consumed the nation for months and toppled one of the hemisphere’s most powerful political parties.

13. Treasury Secretary Jacob J. Lew on April 20 announced the most sweeping and historically symbolic makeover of American currency in a century, proposing to replace the slaveholding Andrew Jackson on the $20 bill with _________, an abolitionist who had been a slave.

14. _________, the songwriter, singer, producer, one-man studio band and consummate showman, died on April 21 at his home, Paisley Park, in Chanhassen, Minn.

15. After Senator Ted Cruz of Texas and Gov. _________ of Ohio, his last Republican rivals, dropped out of the race, Donald J. Trump became the party’s presumptive presidential nominee in May. But some vowed to oppose the party’s new standard-bearer, fueling fears that their party was on the verge of an epochal schism.

16. State laws and federal actions on transgender rights prompted a welter of lawsuits in 2016. In March, the state of _________ passed a law requiring transgender people to use bathrooms in government buildings that correspond with the gender listed on their birth certificates, leading Gov. Pat McCrory and the Justice Department to sue each other. In May, the Obama administration issued a sweeping directive telling every _________ in the country to allow transgender students to use the bathrooms that match their gender identity. In October, the Supreme Court said it would rule on the question.

17. In May, a former director of _________’s antidoping laboratory revealed that dozens of the country’s athletes at the 2014 Winter Olympics in Sochi were part of a state-run doping program. After the disclosures, sports officials began retesting urine samples from the 2008 and 2012 Summer Olympics, in Beijing and London, and their findings have resulted in a top-to-bottom rewriting of Olympic history.

18. A report by the State Department’s inspector general in May sharply criticized Hillary Clinton, saying she was not cleared to use a private _________ for official business when she was secretary of state. In July, the F.B.I. director, _________, recommended no criminal charges against her, lifting an enormous legal cloud from her presidential campaign. But just days before Election Day, the F.B.I. director made a stunning announcement: Agents had discovered a new trove that might be pertinent to the investigation. After the election, Mrs. Clinton cast blame for her loss on this announcement.

19. President Obama in May became the first sitting American president to visit the most potent symbol of the dawning of the nuclear age when he laid a wreath at the _________ Peace Memorial.

20. On May 28, a Cincinnati Zoo employee fatally shot a gorilla named _________ who was manhandling a small boy who had fallen into an enclosure. By the end of the summer, cries for justice for the ape — some earnest and some nonsensical — had spawned a meme with a life all its own.

21. _________, a three-time world heavyweight boxing champion who helped define his turbulent times as the most charismatic and controversial sports figure of the 20th century, died on June 3.

22. On June 12, a gunman killed 49 people and wounded 53 more when he opened fire in a crowded nightclub in _________, making it one of the worst mass shootings in United States history. The shooting touched on some of the most divisive issues in the United States: gay rights, gun control, immigration, the Islamic State and homegrown terrorism, and fears of Islam.

23. LeBron James delivered on the grandest stage of his superhuman career, leading the _________ to the first championship in their history with a 93-89 victory over the Golden State Warriors in Game 7 of the N.B.A. finals on June 19.

24. The Supreme Court handed down several notable decisions in June. It rejected a challenge to a race-conscious admissions program at the University of Texas at Austin, handing supporters of _________ action a major victory. It reaffirmed and strengthened constitutional protections for _________ , striking down parts of a restrictive Texas law that could have drastically reduced the number of clinics in the state. And it announced that it had deadlocked in a case challenging President Obama’s _________ plan, effectively ending what Mr. Obama had hoped would become one of his central legacies.

25. On June 23, Britain voted to leave _________, a decision that set off an immediate wave of geopolitical, economic and cultural shocks. World markets plunged, Prime Minister David Cameron resigned, the British pound fell to a 31-year low, and European leaders began hashing out the logistics of an unprecedented breakup.

26. Three suicide attackers killed at least 41 people and wounded dozens more at _________’s main airport on June 28 in the latest in a string of terrorist attacks in Turkey, a NATO ally once seen as a bastion of stability but now increasingly consumed by the chaos of the Middle East.

27. The Justice Department opened a civil rights investigation into the fatal July 5 shooting of a black man, Alton Sterling, by the Baton Rouge, La., police after a searing video of the encounter reignited contentious issues surrounding police killings of African-Americans. That same week, another black man, Philando Castile, was shot and killed by a police officer during a traffic stop in a suburb of St. Paul. A passenger documented the aftermath in video streamed on _________.

28. Three days later, a retaliatory attack on white police officers shocked the nation as a black Army veteran with a troubled history opened fire on officers patrolling a protest in _________ , killing five officers.

29. On July 14, a Tunisian immigrant with no known radical ties killed 84 people and injured hundreds in the Mediterranean city of _________. He barreled into holiday crowds with a huge rented truck, turning a mainstay of Western commerce into an instrument of mass death.

30. A stunning _________ in Turkey in mid-July left more than 260 people dead, thousands of soldiers rounded up and thousands of judges purged. President Recep Tayyip Erdogan declared a three-month state of emergency and fired or detained tens of thousands of soldiers, civic officials and educators.

31. Donald J. Trump accepted the Republican presidential nomination on July 21 with an unusually vehement appeal to Americans who felt their country was spiraling out of control and yearned for a leader who would take aggressive, even extreme, action to protect them. The Times wrote that “the party he now leads is deeply fractured, with the Washington establishment hewing to positions on trade, immigration and globalism that have been rejected by Mr. Trump and the disaffected _________-collar workers who form much of his base.”

32. _________ became a widespread obsession by mid-July, sending people onto sidewalks and into parks all over the world and spawning anecdotes about people falling off cliffs, discovering dead bodies and wandering unthinkingly into the street.

33. Hillary Clinton, who sacrificed personal ambition for her husband’s political career and then rose to a position of global influence, became the first woman to accept a major party’s presidential nomination on July 28. She acknowledged, however, that many voters still did not relate to her after her eight years as first lady, eight as a senator and four as _________.

34. In a speech that electrified the Democratic convention and turned him into a social media and cable news sensation, _________ waved a pocket Constitution and gave a voice to Muslim Americans outraged by the anti-Muslim pronouncements of the Republican nominee for president, Donald J. Trump. Mr. Trump later belittled this man and his wife, parents of a soldier killed by a suicide bomber in Iraq, and the confrontation emerged as an unexpected flash point in the general election.

35. Brazil’s efforts to stage the Olympics in Rio de Janeiro met trouble at every turn, including polluted _________ and budget cuts so deep that basic operations became strained. But in the end, The Times wrote, the Games exceeded low expectations.

36. Many athletes logged historic victories during the Rio Games, including Michael Phelps, the most decorated Olympian in history, who won his 23rd and final gold medal on Aug. 13; _________, the world’s best gymnast, who won the women’s individual all-around gold medal on Aug. 11; Katie Ledecky, who set world records in the women’s 400-meter and 800-meter freestyle; Simone Manuel, who became the first African-American woman to win an individual swimming event at the Olympics; Katinka Hosszu, the Iron Lady of Hungary, who won three golds in swimming in four days; and _________ of Jamaica, known as the fastest man in the world, who was the first sprinter to win a third gold medal for the third consecutive Olympics.

37. In August, _________, a San Francisco 49ers quarterback, took a knee during the national anthem in a protest against racial injustice and spurred a national debate on race and the definition of patriotism.

38. _________ said on Sept. 9 that it had conducted its fifth underground nuclear test. Since the first test, almost a decade ago, the size of the resulting earthquakes from the country’s test site have increased, indicating that the devices are becoming increasingly powerful.

39. Americans in 2015 reaped the largest economic gains in nearly a generation, the Census Bureau said in September, as poverty fell, health insurance coverage spread and incomes rose sharply for households on every rung of the economic ladder, ending years of stagnation. The gains were an important milestone for the economic expansion that began in 2009, after the Great _________.

40. On Sept. 26, in a relentlessly antagonistic first debate, Donald J. Trump and Hillary Clinton clashed over trade, the Iraq war and his refusal to release his _________, with Mr. Trump frequently showing impatience and political inexperience as Mrs. Clinton pushed him to defend his past denigration of women and President Obama. Watched by 84 million television viewers, it garnered the largest audience since televised debates began in 1960.

41. Colombia was in shock in October after just over 50 percent of voters rejected a peace deal, years in the making, between the government and the leaders of ________, which would have ended 52 years of conflict. Five days after the vote, the president of Colombia was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for pursuing the deal. In late November, however, the government bypassed voters and finally approved a revised peace accord with the rebel group.

42. Nearly 1,000 people died in Haiti as a result of _________. In the United States, at least 21 people were killed, and North Carolina had record flooding.

43. Donald J. Trump set off an uproar in the Republican Party in October after a 2005 recording surfaced that showed him speaking in extraordinarily vulgar terms about _________.

44. On Oct. 13, Bob Dylan was awarded the Nobel Prize in _________. Mr. Dylan, 75, was the first musician to win the award, and his selection was perhaps the most radical choice in a history stretching back to 1901.

45. In the early hours of Nov. 3, the _________ shattered their 108-year championship drought in epic fashion: with an 8-7, 10-inning victory over the Cleveland Indians in Game 7 of the World Series.

46. On Nov. 8, Donald J. Trump was elected the _________th president of the United States in a stunning culmination of an explosive, populist and polarizing campaign that took relentless aim at the institutions and long-held ideals of American democracy. Republicans maintained control of the _________, which meant that the party would control both houses of Congress and the presidency. Hillary Clinton became the second Democratic presidential candidate in modern history to lose despite winning the popular vote, renewing calls from some for doing away with the _________.

47. The nation’s raw divide remained highly visible after the election, with thousands of people turning out to protest in at least 52 cities across the United States. A surge in reports of _________-based attacks also swept the country after Election Day.

48. A crucial two-week _________ conference began on Nov. 7, days after the Paris Agreement went into effect. But problems lay ahead: Only ninety-four of the 195 countries that reached the agreement last year had ratified it, and the United States’ new president-elect has called the issue a hoax.

49. Donald J. Trump reversed course after the election and agreed to pay $25 million to settle a series of lawsuits stemming from his defunct for-profit education venture, _________.

50. In 2016, as _________’s civil war dragged into its sixth year, fragile truces brokered by the United States and Russia crumbled. The war escalated in the fall as government forces and their Russian allies began a ferocious ground assault and aerial bombardment on opposition-held areas of _________, forcing thousands to flee for their lives. In December, President Bashar al-Assad’s forces took full control of the city for the first time since 2012 — a turning point in the war.

51. The United Nations announced in late October that 2016 had been the deadliest year yet for migrants crossing the _________. More than 65 million people have fled their homes to escape conflict or hardship, and the world is in the midst of the largest migration of refugees since World War II.

52. _________, the fiery apostle of revolution who defied the United States for nearly half a century as Cuba’s leader, died on Nov. 25.

53. For months, construction on part of a North Dakota pipeline inspired protests by Native Americans and environmentalists. In early December, the ________ Tribe won a major victory in its battle to block an oil pipeline being built near its reservation when the Department of the Army announced that it would not allow the pipeline to be drilled under a dammed section of the Missouri River.

54. President-elect Donald J. Trump moved to repudiate vast parts of President Obama’s domestic agenda as he began filling his cabinet with conservatives who had long records of opposing the Obama administration on social programs, wages, public lands, veterans and the environment. Among his appointments were _________, a retired neurosurgeon, for secretary of housing and urban development; Betsy DeVos, an avid supporter of school choice, for secretary of education; Senator Jeff Sessions of Alabama for attorney general; and, for his chief strategist, _________, a former head of Breitbart News who critics worry will bring nationalist and racist views to the West Wing.

55. A fire gutted a makeshift nightclub in _________, on Dec. 2, leaving at least 36 people dead in what officials at the scene called one of the worst mass-casualty events in the city’s history.

56. As the year ended, The Times reported on striking parallels between political trends in the United States and Europe: Populist, anti-establishment leaders are rising by tapping into discontent among rural voters against voters who live in _________ and are viewed as elites. This split has been telling in recent elections in Britain, Italy, France, Austria, Lithuania and elsewhere.

57. A vote on Dec. 9 to impeach President Park Geun-hye of _________ now throws both her country and American policy in the region into deep uncertainty, as the North’s nuclear program advances and the incoming administration of Donald J. Trump deliberates over whether to adjust Washington’s stance on how to best contain North Korean aggression.

58. In mid-December, American _________ announced that they had concluded with “high confidence” that Russia had acted covertly in the latter stages of the presidential campaign to harm Hillary Clinton’s chances and promote Donald J. Trump. But an extraordinary breach then emerged between President-elect Trump and the national security establishment, with Mr. Trump mocking assessments that Russia interfered in the election on his behalf, and top Republicans vowing investigations into Kremlin activities. On Dec. 30, President Obama struck back at Russia, ejecting 35 suspected Russian intelligence operatives from the United States and imposing _________ on Russia’s two leading intelligence services.

59. In what The Times called “bookends to a terrible year that saw the wars of the Middle East metastasize across Europe and beyond,” two terrorist attacks — one in Europe, the other on the periphery of Europe — came within hours of each other on Dec. 19. In one, at least 12 people were killed and dozens were wounded when a truck plowed through a _________ in Berlin. In the other, Russia’s _________ to Turkey was assassinated at an Ankara art exhibit by a lone Turkish gunman shouting “God is great!” and “don’t forget Aleppo, don’t forget Syria!”

60. _________, the creamy-voiced English songwriter who sold tens of millions of albums as a member of the duo Wham! and on his own, was found dead on Dec. 25. He was 53.

61. Carrie Fisher, the actress, author and screenwriter who brought a rare combination of nerve, grit and hopefulness to her most indelible role, as _________ in the “Star Wars” movie franchise, died on Dec. 27. She was 60. A day later, her mother, _________ , the wholesome ingénue in 1950s films like “Singin’ in the Rain” and “Tammy and the Bachelor,” died following a stroke. She was 84.
