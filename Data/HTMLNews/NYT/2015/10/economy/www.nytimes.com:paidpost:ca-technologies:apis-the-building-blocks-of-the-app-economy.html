    The Building Blocks of The App Economy

    Think about what you can do today that you could not 15 years ago. Hail a ride or start your car from a smartphone. Find a restaurant from your tablet. Monitor your fitness from a device on your wrist. Check availability of a product and order it from the nearest store anytime, anywhere.

    Or what businesses are doing that they could not in 2000. Expose petabytes of data from legacy systems. Build new applications without reinventing the wheel. Share information instantly with customers, suppliers and partners. Develop. Monetize. In record time.

    There’s something at work behind the scenes that’s powering the new economy, the App Economy. It’s greasing the exchange of information, a modern-day Rosetta Stone.

    It’s called the Application Programming Interface. Scroll down to explore the story of APIs and how two companies are using them to power their digital futures.

    API 101

    APIs are all about sharing. An API is an interface that lets one software program “talk” to another, exchanging data behind the scenes. Sharing has always been a foundation of digital technology. Operating systems, for instance, provide shortcuts that allow programmers to access the keyboard, mouse and other parts of the computer without writing code each time.

    In 2001, companies began sharing Web-based APIs inside and outside their organizations. Since then, they’ve become the operating system of the Web, mobile and Internet of Things. APIs are the connectors of the 21st century, linking previously siloed systems and creating new applications. They’re changing the world by mashing together data and services from upstarts and established companies alike.

    Companies big and small are using APIs to level the playing field. Companies can leverage APIs to expose powerful data from legacy systems to new technologies including smartphones and sensors. Startups and established businesses are bringing in data from partners to add services such as maps and credit card processes to their apps without having to write new code. APIs cut the cost of innovation and entry to new markets.

    While APIs enable many of the apps we use every day, they also turbocharge behind-the-scenes communications between machines. APIs are making it possible for machines to automate processes, predict problems, and generally keep life and businesses humming.

    The adoption and proliferation of APIs could easily create the technological equivalent of the Wild West. API management tools prevent that. From a single interface companies can secure and control access. These gateways also track usage and, if desired, billing. This opens a new revenue stream for many companies.

    Management tools also provide catalogs of available APIs as well as instructions. This allows companies to encourage more developers inside and out to innovate around their APIs and cuts the time it takes to create new services.

    Consumer Impact

    APIs power much of what we do online. Startups and established companies alike are leveraging the technology to become a bigger part of our lives.

    Company Story

    The beauty company L’Oréal USA began embracing APIs two years ago to accelerate its digital transformation. The company needed technology designed to be sharable, flexible, nimble and scalable — enabling L’Oréal to create the personalized digital services today’s connected consumers expect.

    Company Story

    Nearly a dozen APIs now power some of L’Oréal’s key consumer services, enabling a variety of features. They include front-and-center beauty apps, such as Makeup Genius, which uses facial recognition technology to let shoppers virtually try on 420,000 products from lipstick to eyeliner. And behind-the-scenes credit card payment and inventory tracking systems — all aimed at knitting a stronger relationship with customers.

    Company Story

    Three years ago, IceMobile morphed itself from a creative agency into a provider of apps that turbocharge loyalty shopping programs for major retailers. APIs were at the heart of that change. Its Bright Loyalty Platform enables apps to dish up coupons, special offers and digital points that customers can redeem to buy other products, creating a personal relationship that just wasn’t there before.

    APIs help organizations reinvent the way they do business. They provide a more efficient way of building new services. They’re creating markets and closer ties with partners and customers.

    Besides powering apps like Makeup Genius, L’Oréal’s APIs improve partner communication and development because they enable backend systems to more quickly and efficiently share pricing data and product availability across vendors, sites, L’Oréal brands and platforms. APIs provide more agility and cost advantages, too. The company can tap into partners’ merchandising APIs and data.

    By creating loyalty apps tied to what shoppers buy, IceMobile helps retailers increase the amount customers spend by as much as 25 percent compared with traditional paper stamp programs. Participation is also much higher among younger shoppers. APIs provide a way to share data and analytics – even though all companies are running different hardware.

    API Management

    APIs are not inherently secure, making them potential targets for hackers. They’re also proliferating, but that can overwhelm both the providers and consumers of data. API management tools help companies mitigate the risks and realize the benefits of the technology.

    API 101

    The adoption and proliferation of APIs could easily create the technological equivalent of the Wild West. API management tools prevent that. From a single interface companies can secure and control access. These gateways also track usage and, if desired, billing. This opens a new revenue stream for many companies.

    Company Story

    L’Oréal’s API management is the foundation of innovation. It simplifies development and allows for seamless integration. When developers or partners design a new service, they don’t have to build each one from scratch because the APIs allow services and components to be reused. Management tools also provide security, including mechanisms to credential, manage and draw insights from partners and customers. Everything is speaking the same language.

    Company Story

    API management tools are accelerating the pace of innovation and helping reduce costs at IceMobile. The company credits APIs and its management tools with cutting in half the time it takes to get a new retailer up and running. New partners are brought on board in just eight weeks rather than 16 weeks or longer.

    API 101

    Management tools also provide catalogs of available APIs as well as instructions. This allows companies to encourage more developers inside and out to innovate around their APIs and cuts the time it takes to create new services.

    “The biggest hurdle for any company isn’t the legacy technology, but the legacy of your own organization. That’s where APIs and the mentality of making services open help in being better prepared to overcome the legacy you create yourself.”

    Jeroen Pietryga, IceMobile CEO
