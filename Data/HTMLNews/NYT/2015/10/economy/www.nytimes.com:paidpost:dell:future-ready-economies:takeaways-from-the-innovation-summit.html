    Takeaways from the Innovation Summit

    Insights from city leaders, scholars and entrepreneurs who attended the 2015 Innovation Summit on what communities can do to make their economies future ready.

    Imagine if companies had to follow the same rules as the typical U.S. city: New ideas would be endlessly scrutinized by subcommittees and public hearings, and anything experimental would be seen as a risk that could fail, land on newspaper front pages and raise the ire of taxpayers.

    Companies bound by the sometimes‐archaic rules would be on a fast track to the corporate graveyard. Cities, on the other hand, want their economies to be as future ready and innovative as the hottest startup.

    It’s a struggle for most, though a few are succeeding.

    City officials, technologists, entrepreneurs and scholars recently met on the campus of Harvard University to identify those municipal success stories and better understand the secrets of their success. The challenges, they concluded, can be overcome by taking a strategic approach to innovation and finding ways to short‐circuit the legacy processes that hinder progress.

    Insights included the need to find new business models (such as public‐private partnerships), embrace experimentation and manage associated risks, measure usage and ensure that new projects can succeed if they’re deployed broadly.
