    Austin: Innovation
    Texas-Style

    Attracting the best and brightest talent, Austin has an open culture that is helping the city to innovate and solve challenges as it heads into the future.

    Watch the video below.

    TEXAS’S Thriving Capital is world-renowned for its bustling music scene, burgeoning tech industry and tasty food. But those are merely byproducts of a city that not only prizes innovation and openness but also free-spirited out-of-the-box thinking.

    Those factors, which make the city a desirable place to work and live, also helped Austin rank fifth in the Dell Future Ready Economies Model.

    The city is the fastest growing locale in America, with year-over-year population growth of 2.9 percent between July 2013 and July 2014. It’s a testament to how its culture is attracting people and businesses who are bringing more ideas and more innovation deep into the heart of Texas.

    To the people living in Austin, being tagged as a leading future-ready economy doesn’t come as a surprise. “Austin has been growing at a significant percentage,” says Millie Price, managing director of Capital Factory, a workspace and meeting center for Austin’s entrepreneurs. “And the technology industry has been critical to that growth. We’re enabling efficiencies and innovations that’s helping (expand) the city.”

    To Adrian Lipscombe, a project manager for the city and Austin’s bike share coordinator, the reason that the city is such a hub of innovation has less to do with one particular sector or creative scene and more to do with the people and how they interact with one another. “People are allowed to express who they want to be and they don’t take it for granted,” she says. “So if you have an idea and you're an innovator, there are people that are going to be here to support you.”

    Clearly, there’s a lot going on in Austin and Lipscombe has no doubt that this future-ready designation is well deserved. “Anyone can be an entrepreneur here,” she says. “This city has a real feeling to it.”
