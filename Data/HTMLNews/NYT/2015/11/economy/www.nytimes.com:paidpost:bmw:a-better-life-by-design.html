    A Better Life By Design
    How creatives on the frontiers of technology are fashioning our enhanced, elegant future today.
    Work Play Life
    T

    he impending rollout of technology that seemed straight out of science fiction just years ago — automation, artificial intelligence, an untethered workforce with unparalleled tools for communication — suggests we’ll all soon be navigating through a new era of creative career potential.

    The observation

    “the only constant is change” seems quaint considering the forces reshaping our workspaces. Just as open plans, remote collaboration and the entrepreneurial mindset have become norms, new forces are poised to disrupt, such as robot co-workers and the gig economy. But despite the uncertainty, experts believe technology may catalyze a workplace renaissance. The digital revolution has empowered the creative class, studies suggests collaborative workplaces perform better, and a PricewaterhouseCoopers report envisions companies embracing positive social and environmental values.

    Magic Leap

    Google Glass was just the beginning. Augmented reality is being advanced by a host of big and small players alike. Magic Leap is working to layer videos, graphs and data on top of a wearer’s field of vision via a high-tech headset and promises to reshape workplaces everywhere. Imagine welders “seeing” blueprints while working on a construction site, or doctors glancing at charts during a patient consultation. The company’s recent patent filing suggests myriad possibilities, from remote meetings to glasses that turn boring work tasks into virtual games.

    Autonomous Desk

    While the benefits of standing desks have been well publicized, sometimes, a nudge is needed to adopt a healthier habit. If the reminder comes from the desk itself, all the better. The adjustable, AI-enhanced Autonomous Desk adapts to a user, memorizing his or her preferred height, then creates a routine to encourage more standing. But better posture isn’t it’s only performance enhancement: a speaker, wireless charger and voice-activated assistant are built into premium models.

    Genee

    One of the many antiquated office trends showcased in “Mad Men” was the ubiquity of the secretary. Workers in today’s productivity age face more time constraints and pressures without assistants (and can’t duck out like Don Draper). Genee seeks to simplify time management with an AI assistant who, after receiving a text request, takes care of the tedious back-and-forth of coordinating meeting locations and times, even rescheduling if necessary.

    Tylko

    This Polish startup creates cool custom shelving, but the true innovation is personalizing and distributing the manufacturing process. Users design their shelf via an augmented reality app, shaping it on their phone or tablet while “seeing” the shelf in their home or office. The finished blueprint gets sent to a nearby furniture maker (Tylko “rents” unused shop time to distribute the manufacturing process). Tylko just delivers shelves now, but it could help personalize the world of industrial design.

    DUFL

    Today’s business traveler can turn any space into an office, but while traveling, they’re still stuck packing, checking bags and filing overhead bins. Dufl seeks to simplify with a high-tech valet service. Pre-packed personalized luggage can be synced to any itinerary. Dufl packs, folds, ships and stores, eliminating packing and saving time. Dubbed a virtual closet, the service suggests all a jet-setting exec needs to worry about is showing up.

    T

    he elaborate technology that supports the gadgets we use for entertainment could make an aerospace engineer blush; Facebook is about to flight test a massive carbon fiber aircraft in pursuit of better ways to beam Wi-Fi signals to the developing world. But look beyond the technical wizardry, and you’ll see platforms empowering new types of storytelling.

    The internet was supposed

    to make media a niche marketplace. But blockbusters didn’t die; new players changed the definition. YouTube’s five biggest stars have more combined subscribers than Mexico has citizens. The most popular game app, Candy Crush Saga, earns nearly $800,000 a day. GoPro averaged 1,000 portable cameras shipped every hour during fourth quarter 2014. These diverse entertainers and companies all recognize the shift towards personalized entertainment; successful leisure time technology lets users be producers, players and participants.

    The Void

    Virtual reality promises infinite dimensions. But many home headset makers overlook the fact that simulating space may actually become more exciting with some real-world real estate to play with. The Void virtual reality theme park, set to open next year, places users within a huge, domed venue that allows them to walk, explore and encounter obstacles. Players can “see” VR anywhere; with these spacious entertainment centers, they can truly explore.

    Gogoro

    Buying a Gogoro Smartscooter is like getting a bike-share system with serious horsepower. This Taiwanese startup wants to build a cost-efficient transportation network with a network effect, and recently rolled out a series of charging stations to power its new line of high-tech electric motorbikes, which can hit 60 miles per hour. The company is betting riders, who can simply roll up to a smartphone-enabled station and swap batteries when they’re low on power, won’t miss gas stations anytime soon.

    Juse

    Mobile games are expected to generate more revenue than consoles this year, but they’re also likely responsible for more “Game Over” messages due to drained batteries. The solar-powered Juse phone case may give gamers, and everyone else, a perpetual source of extra lives. The crowdfunded passive charging system for smartphones utilizes ultra-efficient solar cells to constantly replenish batteries. If that’s not sustainable enough, part of the cost of each case is put toward funding solar energy projects.

    Zano

    A palm-sized drone that can transmit photos and videos directly to your phone, the Zano could be described as the inevitable evolution of the selfie stick. But more importantly, the Kickstarted device could open up access to drone technology and add new dimensions to personal photography. It’s probably smart the creators included a virtual tether system that connects the mini-copter to a user’s phone, as well as obstacle avoidance technology; if they perform as predicted, it’s probable crowds of these hovering cameras may be sharing the same air space.

    Rocketskates

    Seeing as how the hoverboard became a reality this year, it’s only fitting another Jetsons-esque personal mobility device has hit the market. Strap-on Rocketskates deliver a powerful boost of retro-future entertainment, propelling riders up to 12 m.p.h. The skates offer a few more motor skill challenges than you may encounter at the roller rink, but once mastered, they make cross-town trips easy and won’t hinder movement up and down stairs.

    BMW Gesture Control

    As a renaissance in car technology changes interactions during drivetime, BMW has pioneered a new control system as smooth and seamless as a freshly paved road. Enhancing both safety and usability, this new system (available in the 7 Series) reads a pre-assigned set of hand gestures to truly put the digital aspects of a smart car, such as the stereo, at your fingertips. If you’ve ever conducted music while driving, this innovation is for you.

    T

    he same forces changing our daily lives will reshape how we conceptualize and interact with our homes, resulting in more personalized and interactive spaces that will not only reflect our style, but increasingly, respond to our behavior.

    While we wait for our

    homes to get smarter via the Internet of Things revolution, they’re increasingly becoming more self-sufficient. Recent leaps forward in home energy storage may help households jump off the grid, taking advantage of a forthcoming wave of solar energy advancements. Like our work and leisure time, our home life will become personalized and self-directed: 3D printing, customized manufacturing and indoor farming suggest home will become as much a technology center as a retreat.

    M.Gemi

    Getting handcrafted shoes from Italian cobblers has always been an expensive proposition. E-commerce startup M.Gemi has devised a way to use vertical integration to deliver that same quality, minus the sticker shock. Working with Italian artisans, the company delivers quality directly without middlemen, releasing limited edition designs while utilizing a proprietary measuring system to keep fits and sizes consistent across the entire line, simplifying repeat purchases.

    Zero-Energy Furniture Table

    What if your kitchen table wasn’t just a place for a bowl of cereal in the morning, but also an earth-saving heating and cooling machine? The Zero-Energy Furniture Table, developed by a French designer and engineer, can supposedly cut energy usage by up to 60 percent due to a wax-like substance layered between corrugated aluminum. Set under the table’s surface, it absorbs and releases heat like a sponge. The table functions without sucking up any electricity; stand-up performance for a place to sit down.

    Jibo

    A Wall-E-esque social robot and connection platform, this prototype family digital assistant may begin to make Siri feel jealous. The envy may stem from the character and personality at the heart of this new voice-activated assistant. Along with artificial intelligence and facial recognition, Jibo’s curved shape is meant to suggest a companion, not a servant. And with a developer’s kit and app store in the works, the device may foreshadow a new type of human-computer interaction for the Internet of Things age.

    June

    Want to one-up your friend’s fancy food photos? Why not take a picture straight from the oven? That’s just one of the high-tech tools utilized by the June Intelligent Oven, a consumer-grade cooking computer that also does the work for you. A combination of cameras, sensors and AI helps the smart tabletop convection oven recommend recipes and prepare dishes perfectly, taking guesswork and frustration out of cooking at home.

    Sense

    Predicated in part on the idea that we don’t really analyze sleep — a state that we spend roughly a third of our lives in that directly affects the other two-thirds — this new type of fitness tracker wants to deliver “sleep intelligence.” A bedside monitor and clip-on sensor for your pillow tracks noise, light levels, air quality and the soundness of your night’s rest in an effort to help support better sleeping habits.
