    Your Guide to Augmented Reality in The Times

    By THE NEW YORK TIMES FEB. 1, 2018

    The New York Times introduces the newest advance in digital storytelling. Here are answers to questions about what you will need to use it.

    Augmented reality, introduced in February for iOS, is now available on select Android phones.

    The New York Times has introduced the newest advance in digital storytelling: augmented reality (AR). This technology uses your phone or tablet to create a bridge between the digital world and the physical one. If photography freed journalists to visually capture important moments, and video allowed us to record sight, sound and motion, then our augmented reality feature goes a step further, making flat images three-dimensional. AR brings our report to you in a way that makes it more immediate than ever before.

    Open your NYTimes app to experiment with AR right now. Don’t have the latest NYTimes app? Download it here for Android and here for iPhone and iPad.

    How do I do it?

    iPHONE

    • You will need an iPhone SE, an iPhone 6S or newer, a fifth-generation iPad or an iPad Pro.

    • Make sure you have the latest version of iOS.

    • Check that you have the latest version of the NYTimes app.

    • Allow the NYTimes app to access the camera.

    ANDROID

    • You will need an ARCore-supported phone. Find Google’s official list here.

    • Make sure you have ARCore installed.

    • Check that you have the latest version of the NYTimes app.

    • Allow the NYTimes app to access the camera.

    What if I don’t have a supported device?

    We have created immersive features meant for viewing on a desktop or mobile web, so just look at an article the way you normally would.

    Why was AR available on iOS before Android?

    The Times launched AR in February for iOS users, built on Apple’s ARKit technology. ARCore, which was part of our plan for building AR experiences for Android, has since been released. So we are now able to offer AR features to readers who use supported Android phones.

    Why do I need to allow access to my camera?

    Your camera allows us to render 3-D images in the space in front of you. We do not have the ability to peer through your lens, to track your location or even to save what you’re viewing. For more information, see our privacy policy and terms of service. If you would like to reverse the decision to let us access the camera on your Android phone, go to Settings > Apps > NYTimes > Permissions, and then toggle the button.

    On an iOS device, go to Settings > Privacy > Camera > NYTimes and then toggle the button.

    How do I have the best AR experience?

    Make sure you are in a well-lit area, with room in front of you. Point your phone at a nonreflective surface, like a rug — not glass. Then, move your phone in a slow, circular motion to help the AR image render. Once you see the image, here are a few notes:

    • To get a closer look, walk up to it and around it. You can bend down and look up too. (Pinching your screen to zoom won’t do anything.)

    • Be aware of your surroundings as you move around the 3-D rendering.

    • Do not use while in motion, like on a train or in an elevator.

    • Don’t be alarmed if your phone gets warm during use. This can happen with certain technology.

    Need help? Contact us.
