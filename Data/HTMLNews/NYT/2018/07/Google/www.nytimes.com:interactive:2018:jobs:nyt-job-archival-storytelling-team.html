    Join the Archival Storytelling Team at The New York Times

    JULY 11, 2018

    The New York Times is building a dynamic creative team to dream up new ways to resurface our rediscovered visual treasures.

    New York Times photographer Ernie Sisto with camera and huge lens in September 1947. The New York Times

    Join the Archival Storytelling Team at The New York Times

    The New York Times is building a dynamic creative team to dream up new ways to resurface our rediscovered visual treasures as we digitize the hundreds of thousands of photographs in our vast archives. We would like a team to exhume the photographs and stories that had been relegated to the dustbins of history and to explore anew the stories we left untold. The team’s charge will be to to create projects and series that wed previous coverage with new reporting and sleek digital presentation, akin to efforts like Overlooked and Unpublished Black History.

    This ambitious project will require a number of roles over the course of one year. They are as follows:

    • Editorial Lead, Archival Storytelling

      We are seeking an imaginative editor to develop and manage a team devoted to archival storytelling. This editor should be passionate about history and should have experience collaborating with a cross-functional team of photo editors, digital designers, writers and social media editors. The editor will also assign stories, manage staff resources, appear at Times events and do external outreach to bring visibility to this project and to explore editorial partnerships. The ideal candidate will have five years of experience as an editor for a general consumer publication, will have visual aptitude and a familiarity with 20th century history. The editor should also be a story diagnostician not afraid to get her hands wet. This is a temporary excluded position that is expected to last about 1 year. This role reports to Monica Drake, the assistant managing editor who manages special projects.

      Requirements

      +Ten years creating editorial content.

      +Experience managing a content team that draws on visual editors.

      +Familiarity with resurfacing content across digital platforms.

      +Experience with audience-led editorial planning

      +A varied portfolio of editorial projects

      +Facility with editorial cms’s that incorporate images and other media.

      Apply here.

    • Senior Staff Editor, Archival Storytelling

      The archival storytelling team requires an experienced, insightful and detail-oriented line editor who can bring stories across the finish line. This editor should be able to juggle several elements of a story, including pithy deep captions, display type, social language, and should be able to make deadlines for all platforms, from print to Instagram to newsletters. And the editor should have experience working on visual-first story forms.

      This is a temporary guild position that is expected to last approximately 1 year and is part of the Special Projects team.

      Requirements

      +5 years of experience copy editing or line editing at a major consumer publication

      +Familiarity with varied digital platforms

      +Facility with editorial cms that incorporates images and other media.

      +Experience working on a collaborative team that involves visual editors

      Apply here.

    • Reporter, Archival Storytelling

      We are seeking a writer and history buff familiar with revisiting long-forgotten narratives to tell stories inspired by the photographic gems we recover as we digitize our archives. This writer should have experience working at a general consumer publication on deadline, should be fastidious in verifying facts with primary sources and should know how to research topics that may elude a simple Google search. The ideal candidate is also a reporter who is an expert at drawing tales from scholars, historians and people who are revisiting moments from their past. This is a temporary guild position that is expected to last approximately 1 year and is part of the Special Projects team.

      Requirements

      +5 years of experience reporting and writing at a general interest publication

      +Familiarity with digital platforms

      +Has collaborated with a photo editor or other visual editor to write stories.

      +Experience researching historical records

      Apply here.

    • Researcher, Archival Storytelling

      We are looking for an experienced researcher who is an expert at finding nuggets embedded in historic records to assist our archival storytelling team. The ideal candidate should have worked on an editorial team on tight deadlines, is familiar with digital research tools, and specializes in poring over archival photography to unearth clues about images. This person should also be well versed in 20th century history and well acquainted with New York City’s history, too.

      This is a temporary guild position that is expected to last approximately 1 year and is part of the Special Projects team.

      Requirements

      +Experience researching historic facts for a media publication

      +Familiarity with digital research tools.

      +Facility with archival history.

      +Experience using photography to frame research

      Apply here.

    • Staff Editor, Photo Department, Archival Storytelling

      We are seeking an experienced photo editor to help us tell visually driven stories inspired by our rediscovered archival images. We’d like an editor who can retrieve photographs to illustrate a reported story and who can dream up ideas for articles told mainly through pictures.

      The ideal candidate should have a passion for photography, journalism and visual storytelling of all kinds. A firm sense of journalistic ethics is essential, as is a knowledge of photographic usage and copyright issues.

      This editor must be a motivated and detail-oriented self-starter with an ability to complete tasks and meet deadlines with minimal supervision. The candidate must also be hyper-organized, a team player and an excellent communicator.

      This is a temporary guild position that is expected to last approximately 1 year and is embedded with the Special Projects team. It reports to Nakyung Han, Photo Department Editor.

      Requirements

      +3-5 years of experience working on an editorial project and cross-functional team.

      +Experience working with archival images.

      +Has created content based on still images.

      +Familiarity with web publishing tools

      Apply here.

    • Staff Editor, Photo Department - Archival Scanning

      We are seeking an experienced photo editor to help us tell visually driven stories inspired by our rediscovered archival images. We’d like an editor with a strong photo research background who can retrieve photographs to illustrate a reported story and who can see story potential in our collection.

      The ideal candidate should have a passion for photography, journalism and visual storytelling of all kinds. A firm sense of journalistic ethics are essential, as is a knowledge of photographic usage and copyright issues.

      This editor must be a motivated and detail-oriented self-starter with an ability to complete tasks and meet deadlines with minimal supervision. The candidate must also be hyper-organized, a team player and an excellent communicator.

      This is a temporary guild position that is expected to last approximately 1 year and is embedded with the Special Projects team. It reports to Nakyung Han, Photo Department Editor.

      Requirements

      +3-5 years of experience working on an editorial project and cross-functional team.

      +Experience working with archival images and negatives.

      +Has created content based on still images.

      +Familiarity with electronic image handling and editing.

      Apply here.

    • Project Manager, Archival Scanning

      We are hiring a project manager to oversee the digitization of our archival image collection. The project manager will coordinate all facets of the digitization, including planning, establishing workflows, managing personnel on scanning team and scheduling. This person will also be responsible for coordinating work with other relevant teams.Think start-up!

      The ideal candidate must be motivated, detail-oriented and shouldw be an excellent communicator with an ability to inspire and help lead the team.

      Prior experience and a familiarity with editorial photography and electronic image handling is required. A facility for learning new technology is essential.

      This is a one-year contractual position with the Photo Desk and reports to Nakyung Han, Photo Department Editor.

      Requirements

      +Familiarity with project management tools like DropBox paper and Trello.

      +Experience managing communication and aligning priorities with multiple teams.

      +Has served as the lead project manager of a multi-faceted project.

      Apply here.

    • Technician, Archival Scanning

      The New York Times is looking to hire 6 image scanning technicians to help digitize our archival print image collection.

      The technicians will perform essential functions, including: conducting a pre-scan assessment of images in our collection, facilitating and performing scanning, ensuring proper metadata input and accurate caption transfer, organizing and refiling hard-copy images to our storage area after completion of scanning.

      The ideal candidate must be a motivated and detail-oriented self-starter with an ability to complete tasks and meet deadlines with minimal supervision. This person must also be hyper-organized, a team player and an excellent communicator.

      Requirements

      +Experience and a familiarity with print handling and electronic image handling.

      +Facility for learning new technology.

      This is a temporary guild position that is expected to last approximately 1 year. It reports to Nakyung Han, Photo Department Editor.

      Apply here.

    • Archival Assistant, Scanning

      The New York Times is seeking an assistant to help digitize our archival print image collection.

      The assistant will be a lynchpin of the scanning team, with responsibilities including: assisting an archivist in taking inventory of the image collection, facilitating workflow for scanning, conducting pre-scan assessment of images, organizing and refiling hard-copy images to our physical storage space after scanning.

      The assistant must be a motivated and detail-oriented self-starter with an ability to complete tasks and meet deadlines with minimal supervision. The ideal candidate is hyper-organized, a team player and an excellent communicator.

      This position will report into the Project Manager, Archival Scanning and will be embedded on the archival scanning team within our Photo Desk. This is a temporary position expected to last about year.

      Requirements

      +Experience and a familiarity with print handling and electronic image handling.

      +Facility for learning new technology is essential.

      Apply here.
