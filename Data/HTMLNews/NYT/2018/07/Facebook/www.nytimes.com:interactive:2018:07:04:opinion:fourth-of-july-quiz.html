    America, the Quiz

    By KEITH WILLIAMS and LIRIEL HIGA JULY 4, 2018

    Just how well do you know the United States?

    Happy Fourth of July! As you tuck into hot dogs and burgers while waiting for fireworks, test your knowledge of all things Americana.

    1 of 10

    Although today we celebrate July 4 as Independence Day, the Continental Congress actually voted to adopt the Declaration of Independence on what date in 1776?

    May 30

    June 14

    July 2

    2 of 10

    The American Library Association is dropping the name of which author from a prestigious children’s literature award?

    Dr. Seuss

    E. B. White

    Laura Ingalls Wilder

    3 of 10

    Woody Guthrie wrote “This Land Is Your Land” as a response to which song?

    “God Bless America”

    “Battle Hymn of the Republic”

    “America the Beautiful”

    4 of 10

    In 2017, Joey Chestnut set a record at the Nathan’s Hot Dog Eating Contest, downing 72 dogs in 10 minutes. The women’s record of 45, set in 2012, belongs to what competitor, known as the “Black Widow”?

    Molly Schuyler

    Miki Sudo

    Sonya Thomas

    5 of 10

    Spiro T. Agnew was the second vice president to resign, in 1973. The first was what states' rights activist who stepped down in 1832 to represent South Carolina in the Senate?

    John C. Calhoun

    Henry Clay

    Daniel Webster

    6 of 10

    In what year did the United States Census Bureau declare that the frontier no longer existed?

    1870

    1890

    1910

    7 of 10

    What question, posed to Senator Joseph McCarthy by the Army lawyer Joseph Welch in 1954, is often cited as the unraveling point of McCarthy’s anti-Communist campaign?

    “Have you lost your mind?”

    “Have you no sense of decency?”

    “What do you see when you look in the mirror?”

    8 of 10

    What victim of the 1770 Boston Massacre, a sailor of African and Native American descent, is considered by many historians to be the first fatality of the American Revolution?

    Crispus Attucks

    James Caldwell

    Samuel Maverick

    9 of 10

    Which former first daughter wrote this about her father?

    “He would be appalled and heartbroken at a Congress that refuses to stand up to a president who not only seems ignorant of the Constitution but who also attempts at every turn to dismantle and mock our system of checks and balances.”

    Caroline Kennedy

    Susan Ford

    Patti Davis

    10 of 10

    What was notable about Mary Doyle Keefe, who died in 2015 at age 92?

    She was the first female NASA engineer

    She participated in D-Day disguised as a man

    She was the model for “Rosie the Riveter”

    Keith Williams writes the F.Y.I. column for The Times. He won the 2003 Jeopardy! College Championship and this year won $50,000 on “Who Wants to Be a Millionaire." Liriel Higa is a staff editor in the Opinion section and watches Jeopardy! with her grandmother.
