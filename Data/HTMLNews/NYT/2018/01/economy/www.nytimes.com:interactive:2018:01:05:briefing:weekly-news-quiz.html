    News Quiz: Test Your Knowledge of the Week’s Headlines

    By CHRIS STANFORD and ANNA SCHAVERIEN JAN. 5, 2018

    It's a new year, but we're bringing back an old feature: the weekly news quiz. Did you stay on top of the most important stories this week? Test your knowledge of major news headlines and featured articles from across the globe. To take the quiz, click on an answer, and the correct response will be revealed after you choose.
    Click here for other weeks’ quizzes.

    1 of 11

    We’ll start with an easy one: In a performance in Times Square on New Year’s Eve, which singer redeemed herself after a live-television fiasco the year before?

    Cardi B

    Mariah Carey

    Lady Gaga

    Taylor Swift

    2 of 11

    In a speech on New Year’s Day, North Korea’s leader, Kim Jong-un, offered to send an Olympic delegation to the Winter Games in South Korea next month. Which city is hosting the Olympics?

    Busan

    Incheon

    Pyeongchang

    Seoul

    3 of 11

    President Trump broke from his onetime chief strategist, Stephen Bannon, this week after Mr. Bannon was quoted in a new book disparaging the president’s children, asserting that Donald Trump Jr. had been “treasonous” to meet with Russians.

    The book, published on Friday, has the subtitle “Inside the Trump White House.” What is its title?

    “The Deplorables”

    “Fire and Fury”

    “American Carnage”

    “The Art of the Comeback”

    4 of 11

    The protests in Iran this week were driven partly by concerns over the country’s poor economy, which especially affects young people. According to official statistics, what percent of Iran’s population is under the age of 30?

    75 percent

    50 percent

    30 percent

    15 percent

    On Monday, California became the sixth state in the U.S. to introduce the sale of recreational marijuana. Which of these states is not among them?

    Alaska

    Colorado

    Massachusetts

    Nevada

    Also on Monday, Bill de Blasio, a Democrat, was sworn in for his second and final term as the mayor of New York City. When was the last time the city re-elected a Democratic mayor?

    1969

    1985

    1993

    2005

    7 of 11

    The local media called it a “movie-worthy heist.” On Wednesday, thieves made off with a gold brooch and earrings belonging to a member of the Qatari royal family. The jewels, which had a declared value of about $36,000, were stolen from an exhibition in which city?

    Doha

    Las Vegas

    Milan

    Venice

    8 of 11

    “I’ve always been a fighter. I was an amateur boxer in my youth, and I’ve brought that fighting spirit with me to Washington. But every good fighter knows when to hang up the gloves. And for me, that time is soon approaching.”

    Who said it?

    Representative Jason Chaffetz of Utah

    Representative Charlie Dent of Pennsylvania

    Senator Orrin Hatch of Utah

    Representative Ileana Ros-Lehtinen of Florida

    9 of 11

    As part of the long-running bull market, the Standard & Poor’s 500-stock index gained 19 percent in 2017.

    The index also experienced a low level of volatility, fluctuating by no more than how many percentage points on any given day?

    1.2 percent

    2 percent

    2.7 percent

    3 percent

    10 of 11

    According to The Times’s review, “The stakes could not have been higher. The chaos could not have been wilder. It’s fair to say that no production in the Metropolitan Opera’s history has been more vexed than the new staging of” what?

    “Cavalleria Rusticana & Pagliacci”

    “The Exterminating Angel”

    “Le Nozze di Figaro”

    11 of 11

    Under Coach Nick Saban, the Alabama Crimson Tide has played in five college football championship games. How many did the team win?

    5

    4

    3

    2

    Correction: Jan. 7, 2018

    An earlier version of this quiz misstated the number of national championship games that Alabama has played under head coach Nick Saban, and the number the team has won. Both have been corrected.
