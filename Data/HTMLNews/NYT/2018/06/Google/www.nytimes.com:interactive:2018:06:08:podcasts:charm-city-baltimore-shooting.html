    ‘Charm City’: A Family Searches for Answers After a Fatal Police Shooting in Baltimore

    JUNE 8, 2018

    A five-part audio series about a Baltimore teenager who was killed by the police and a grieving family still searching for answers.

    Part One

    Baltimore After Freddie Gray

    A year after the death of Freddie Gray, a teenager known as Nook was shot and killed by a police officer. Nook’s family seeks truth from the streets where he died and the city that won’t give them answers.

    Davetta at the library. Miranda Barnes for The New York Times
    Nook’s little cousins. Miranda Barnes for The New York Times

    Part Two

    The Legacy of Zero-Tolerance Policing

    Relations between the police and the community in Baltimore weren’t always so troubled. But as job loss and drugs tore through the city, a policing idea called zero tolerance led to a breakdown in trust.

    Read the transcript
    Nook’s room. Miranda Barnes for The New York Times

    Part Three

    The Lure of the Streets

    Nook spent the first years of his life in an affluent suburb. But when he returned to Baltimore, he became part of a young generation caught between the crack epidemic and the aggressive police tactics meant to fix the problem.

    Read the transcript
    A Baltimore street corner. Miranda Barnes for The New York Times
    Balloons in the street commemorating Nook’s birthday. Miranda Barnes for The New York Times

    Part Four

    The Police Scandal That Shook Baltimore

    Nook’s mother, Toby, had long ago lost faith in the Baltimore police. And as the department tried to repair its public image, a corruption trial exposed the depths of misconduct: an elite group of officers had been stealing from residents.

    Part Five

    What’s Behind the Black Box?

    Nook’s family thinks they know who killed him. But when the Baltimore police finally agree to show Toby the full surveillance video of his death, a new story emerges.
