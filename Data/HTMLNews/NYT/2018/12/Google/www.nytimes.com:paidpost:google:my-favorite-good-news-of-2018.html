    of 2018

    Kacey Musgraves, Angela Duckworth,
    Chloe Kim and José Andrés
    discuss some of the most inspiring
    moments of the year.

    When a young man scaled the side of a building in Paris on May 27, 2018, to rescue a child in danger, the world watched in awe. Immediately after, Google searches for “paris hero” skyrocketed across the globe. Similarly, while the world looked on in suspense as 12 boys and their football coach awaited rescue from a cave in Thailand, searches for “best cave divers in the world” increased five-fold. The positive headlines from 2018 not only captivated us, they inspired what we searched for, from the everyday (“soccer player haircuts”) to the history-making (“how many women in congress”).

    The world searched for “good” in 2018 more than ever before, according to Google Trends. Online and in headlines, we looked for reasons to smile, ways to help, and milestones to inspire.

    To celebrate this universal search for good news, we've asked four familiar faces to share their favorite headlines this year from The New York Times. Those who reflected on their favorite good news moments are: Kacey Musgraves, the award-winning singer and songwriter; Angela Duckworth, a psychologist and author of “Grit: The Power of Passion and Perseverance”; Chloe Kim, a gold-medal winner in snowboarding; and José Andrés, a chef and chairman of World Central Kitchen.

    Kacey Musgraves’s favorite headline

    European Parliament Approves Ban on Single-Use Plastics

    European Parliament Approves Ban on Single-Use Plastics

    — The New York Times, Oct. 25, 2018,
    Read More

    — The New York Times, Oct. 25, 2018, Read More

    “I’ve recently been vigilant in my own life about reducing the number of single-use plastics I take part in using. My sister woke me up to the absurdity and environmental danger of it all. I hadn’t pondered it even while touring and using countless bottles of water, disposable cutlery and temporary food packaging.

    Big change can’t happen unless small change can happen first. I feel an urgency to do what I can, even in small ways, to help this planet we call home. I’m still not the best at it, but I’m trying. I feel overwhelmed when I see the damage that’s been done to our oceans and animals. Eventually this will affect everything we know.

    The touring industry does not lend itself to being environmentally conscious. But there are many things we can all do to help, just by changing small habits.

    Despite its problems, I am so inspired by this place in the universe we call home. Since it’s done so much to serve me I want to do my part to serve it before it’s truly too late.”

    Angela Duckworth’s favorite headline

    For Just the Third Time in 117 Years, a Woman Wins the Nobel Prize in Physics

    For Just the Third Time in 117 Years, a Woman Wins the Nobel Prize in Physics

    — The New York Times, Oct. 2, 2018,
    Read More

    — The New York Times, October 2, 2018, Read More

    “I think it would be wonderful if girls everywhere knew that the stereotype that boys are outperforming them academically is not true.

    I have studied gender differences and achievement, and girls do better than boys in all academic subjects, including the hard sciences and mathematics. When you look at their grades, at every level of education, girls do better.

    Why are girls getting better grades? They work harder in school on average. Why doesn't anybody know about it? Well, that's probably at least as much a sociological question as it is a psychological one.”

    (One of) Chloe Kim’s favorite headlines

    How Rescuers in a Thai Cave Pulled Off the Impossible

    How Rescuers in a Thai Cave Pulled Off the Impossible

    — The New York Times, July 10, 2018, Read More

    “This was my favorite headline of the year, because this story was insane to me. I’m so glad the kids were saved!

    I remember reading about it and following along for days as the rescuers tried to figure out how to get the kids out. I thought it was crazy and so scary! All of those kids being stuck in a cave for so long and almost drowning is absolutely terrifying. Hearing that they were rescued and made it out alive was incredible.

    This was a particularly special year for me. Winning a gold medal was, hands down, the highlight of my 2018 and probably the biggest highlight of my whole life.”

    During the two weeks in which the boys were trapped, Google searches for “scuba diving lessons” nearly doubled in the United States.

    José Andrés’s favorite headline

    Bans on Plastic Straws in Restaurants Expand to More Cities

    Bans on Plastic Straws in Restaurants Expand to More Cities

    — The New York Times, March 3, 2018,
    Read More

    — The New York Times, March 3, 2018, Read More

    “I’ve fallen in love with scuba diving, especially with turtles. I’ve dived with many animals, but I’m happiest when I'm surrounded by turtles. Nothing gave me more of a call to action than the 2015 photo and video of the turtle with the plastic straw stuck in its nose.

    Every single action in the process of feeding ourselves has a huge consequence on the outside world. Most of the time we are eating without thinking. Around 2010, we took plastic straws out of many of our restaurants. I'd already begun reading about the negative impact the plastic has on the ocean.

    We started bringing the drinks without a straw and only gave a straw if people requested it, as a way of minimizing the damage and still trying to be a catalyst of change.

    It takes a global community to make something happen. It takes a village to make change.”
