    The Culture Calendar

    AUG. 29, 2018

    Plan ahead for the season to come. Email yourself an event reminder or subscribe here to receive our monthly updates.

    Mar 4 -

    Mar 10

    Dave Sanders for The New York Times

    art

    Weekend: The Armory Show

    Runs March 7-10 at Piers 90 and 94. One of the city’s biggest art fairs takes over Piers 90 and 94 in Manhattan (after its usual home at Pier 92 was found to be structurally unsound). Last year, the show drew an estimated 65,000 visitors, with work by artists from 31 countries. Buy tickets ahead to skip the lines.

    Tom Jamieson for The New York Times

    theater

    Get Tix: ‘Fleabag’

    Opens Thursday, March 7. Phoebe Waller-Bridge is bringing her breakout stage show-turned-television hit “Fleabag” to New York for an Off Broadway run at the SoHo Playhouse. Her one-person show runs through April 14. Tickets here.

    theater

    Get Tix: Suzan-Lori Parks’s ‘White Noise’

    Previews begin Tuesday, March 5 at The Public Theater. No American playwright has contributed more to the conversation about race in these United (and divided) States than Suzan-Lori Parks, the imaginative writer who won a Pulitzer Prize for “Topdog/Underdog.” Oskar Eustis of The Public oversees a cast led by Daveed Diggs (Thomas Jefferson and the Marquis de Lafayette in the original cast of “Hamilton”) as an African-American man forced to reconsider the depth and danger of the gap between black and white. Tickets here.

    Ana Cuba for The New York Times

    pop

    Get Tix: Robyn

    Friday, March 8 at Madison Square Garden. A Robyn concert isn’t an over-the-top spectacle of rotating set pieces and wardrobe changes, or a showcase of vocal acrobatics and choreographed backup dancers. It is sweaty and straight-up — pop church and pop group therapy. Read our profile here. Tickets here.

    Sara Krulwich/The New York Times

    theater

    Last Chance: ‘Choir Boy’

    Runs through Sunday, March 10. Tarell Alvin McCraney, the playwright who won an Oscar for “Moonlight,” made his Broadway debut with “Choir Boy,” a story that centers on a boys’ prep school and its gospel choir. An NYT Critic’s Pick. Read our review here. Tickets here.

    Mar 11 -

    Mar 17

    Sara Krulwich/The New York Times

    theater

    Get Tix: ‘What the Constitution Means to Me’

    Previews begin Thursday, March 14. Heidi Schreck’s play about invisible women and the 14th Amendment was electrifyingly topical when it ran Off Broadway last year. The play feels even more urgent as it moves to Broadway, with its take on abortion as a protected liberty. Read Ben Brantley’s review here. Tickets here.

    Claudette Barius/Universal Pictures

    film

    In Theaters: Jordan Peele’s ‘Us’

    Opens Friday, March 15. Jordan Peele’s newest horror-thriller follows a family terrorized by what seem to be evil doppelgängers of themselves. With Lupita Nyong’o and Winston Duke. Watch the trailer here.

    theater

    Get Tix: ‘Kiss Me, Kate’ at Studio 54

    Opens Friday, March 15. Brush up on your Shakespeare and your Cole Porter, too, because the Roundabout is reviving the Tony Award-winning musical take on “The Taming of the Shrew.” Kelli O’Hara, one of Broadway’s most beloved contemporary performers, stars as Kate. Tickets here.

    pop

    Get Tix: Massive Attack

    March 15 and 16 at Radio City. British trip-hop group Massive Attack celebrates the 20th(ish) anniversary of its album “Mezzanine” with a brief U.S. tour that promises to be a visual extravaganza. Enjoy this throwback to their 1998 tour: Tickets here.

    Sara Krulwich/The New York Times

    theater

    Last Chance: ‘True West’

    Runs through Sunday, March 17. Ethan Hawke and Paul Dano star in this revival of Sam Shepard’s explosive classic about sibling rivalry. An NYT Critic’s Pick. Read our review here. Tickets here.

    Mar 18 -

    Mar 24

    Sara Krulwich/The New York Times

    theater

    Get Tix: ‘Oklahoma!’ on Broadway

    Opens Tuesday, March 19. After a sold-out run at St. Ann’s Warehouse, this darkly revisionist revival of Rodgers and Hammerstein’s “Oklahoma!” is coming to Broadway. “Likely to be the freshest, most revolutionary musical on Broadway this season,” writes Ben Brantley. Read our review here. Tickets here.

    film

    In Theaters: ‘Where’d You Go, Bernadette’

    Opens Friday, March 22. Richard Linklater brings Maria Semple’s novel to the big screen, starring Cate Blanchett in the title role, for a skewering of Seattle’s Microsoft elite. Read Janet Maslin’s review of the book here. Watch the trailer here.

    Mark Douet

    theater

    Get Tix: ‘The Lehman Trilogy’

    Previews begin Friday, March 22 at The Armory. An NYT Critic’s pick when it ran in London, this epic tale of financial rise and ruin stars Simon Russell Beale, Ben Miles and Adam Godley — three seemingly inexhaustible actors, with the power to multiply themselves like amoebas. “Behold them with wonder, humble theatergoer, for they are multitudes,” writes Ben Brantley. Tickets here.

    Mar 25 -

    Mar 31

    pop

    Last Chance: KISS ‘Farewell’ Tour

    Wednesday, March 27 at MSG. Almost 20 years after “One Last KISS: End of the Road,” the very tonguey and very cheeky band returns for another “last” goodbye. Tickets here.

    Daniel Arnold for The New York Times

    tv

    Series Finale: ‘Broad City’

    Thursday, March 28 on Comedy Central. Join BFFs Abbi and Ilana for the final episode of their stoned millennial romp through New York City. Watch the trailer for their farewell season here.

    film

    In Theaters: Tim Burton’s ‘Dumbo’

    Opens Friday, March 29. Burton directs a new version of Disney’s 1941 tear-jerker with a cast that includes Danny DeVito and Michael Keaton. Watch the trailer here.

    Julieta Cervantes

    theater

    Get Tix: ‘Tootsie’ the Musical

    Previews begin Friday, March 29. “I have high hopes for the stage version of ‘Tootsie,’” writes Jesse Green. The musical comedy, based on the 1982 Dustin Hoffman movie, will feature songs written by David Yazbek, the man behind for “Dirty Rotten Scoundrels” and “The Full Monty.” Tickets here.

    The Andy Warhol Foundation for the Visual Arts, Inc./Licensed by Artists Rights Society (ARS), New York; Rebecca Smeyne for The New York Times

    art

    Last Chance: ‘Andy Warhol’ at The Whitney

    Runs through Sunday, March 31. The iconic soup cans, among 350 of the artist’s works, will soon be packed up after one of the blockbuster shows of the season comes to a close. Warhol “reasserts his power for a new generation,” Holland Cotter writes. Read his review here. Fridays are pay what you wish, 7-10 p.m. More visiting info here.

    Apr 1 -

    Apr 7

    tv

    Premiere: Jordan Peele’s ‘Twilight Zone’ Reboot

    Debuts Monday, April 1 on CBS. “The Twilight Zone,” the show that captivated audiences in the early 1960s and beyond with its time-and-space-bending plots, will return to (streaming) television in a reboot helmed by Jordan Peele. It will air on CBS All Access, as that subscription-based streaming service tries to grow its stable of original programming. Watch the trailer here.

    Tony Cenicola/The New York Times

    pop

    Get Tix: The Shed

    Opens April 5. The Shed, the new cross-disciplinary arts center (and feat of architectural engineering) opens with a flight of ambitious programming: “Soundtrack of America,” a celebration of African American music directed by Oscar-winning filmmaker Steve McQueen; an elaborately-staged Björk concert; and “Norma Jeane Baker of Troy,” a dramatic work by Ann Carson, performed by Renée Fleming. Look ahead to June/July for a kung fu musical with remixed songs by Sia. Tickets here.

    Sara Krulwich/The New York Times

    theater

    Last Chance: ‘The Band’s Visit’

    Final performance April 7. The Tony-winning musical about an imaginary encounter between Arab musicians and Jewish villagers in a small Israeli desert town, will end its Broadway run. Ben Brantley called it “one of the most ravishing musicals you will ever be seduced by.” Read his review here. Tickets here.

    Sara Krulwich/The New York Times

    theater

    Last Chance: ‘Kinky Boots’

    Final performance April 7. The Cyndi Lauper-Harvey Fierstein collaboration that won the best new musical Tony Award in 2013 will come to an end, making it one of the longest-running productions in Broadway history. Read Ben Brantley's original review here. Tickets here.

    Apr 14

    Helen Sloane/HBO

    tv

    Premiere: ‘Game of Thrones’ Final Season

    Returns Sunday, April 14 on HBO. 564 days since “Game of Thrones” last aired a new episode, the sci-fi epic that changed television returns for its eight and final season. Get caught up here.

    Apr 15 -

    Apr 21

    Jack Mitchell/Getty Images

    dance

    One Night Only: Merce Cunningham’s ‘100 Solos’

    Tuesday, April 16 at BAM. Merce Cunningham, that marvel of a choreographer, understood that dancing meant different things to different people. In 2019, the year of his centennial, Cunningham — who died at 90 in 2009 — is being celebrated all over the world with performances, workshops, talks, screenings and more. One highlight is just around the corner: “Night of 100 Solos: A Centennial Event,” on April 16, Cunningham’s birthday. Tickets here.
