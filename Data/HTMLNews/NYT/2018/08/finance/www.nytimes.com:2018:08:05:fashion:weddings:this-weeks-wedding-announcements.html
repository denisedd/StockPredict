Jeanie Knigin and Gary Knisely

Jeanie Walls Knigin and William Gary Knisely met in 2016, after a woman whom Ms. Knigin knew referred Mr. Knisely to her as a potential date. It turned out that he had been out with several other women in Ms. Knigin’s sphere, but none had been quite right, and each had suggested that he might contact another acquaintance.

“It was really a women’s network thing,” she said. “I was No. 4.”

After their first date, on a Friday, they agreed to meet again the following Tuesday.

“He’s funny, he’s handsome, he’s smart, his eyes — I really like his eyes,” Ms. Knigin said. “I don’t know, he’s the whole package.”

But then Ms. Knigin received an email from Mr. Knisely saying that he wouldn’t be able to meet again after all. He’d been seeing someone else and had mistakenly thought that the relationship had concluded.

“I was really disappointed, I thought we had something,” she said. “I had been a widow, had been on Match, eHarmony, and I finally just sort of gave up.”

Six months later, Mr. Knisely contacted Ms. Knigin again. This time, he said, the other relationship was truly over, and Ms. Knigin had been on his mind since that first date.

“She’s just the nicest person in the world,” he said. “She happens to be very beautiful, but what sticks is that she’s just honest, straightforward, wasn’t complicated, is very caring. All those wonderful human qualities, that’s what got me.”

Jaclyn Ambriscoe and Richard Hagner

Capt. Richard Williamson Hagner spotted Capt. Jaclyn Georgette Ambriscoe in classes during two weeks of preliminary training at Fort Lee, in Virginia, in February 2013, before heading to Charlottesville, Va., to study military law for eight weeks in the Judge Advocate General’s Legal Center and School. He said he noticed her for two reasons.

“First obviously she’s very pretty,” Captain Hagner said, “and her last name was at the top of the alphabet and she sat at the front of the class.”

Although Captain Ambriscoe considered him “good-looking, gentlemanly and polite,” when she briefly chatted with him at the food court on the Fort Lee base, she did not take him up on his offer to sit with him.

“I was focused on wearing my uniform correctly or learning how to salute,” she said.

She eased up in Charlottesville, where they began hanging out in the same group for meals and outings, and when her parents took him and some of her friends to lunch after graduation she recalled her mother whispering in her ear.

“My mother thought he was the one for me,” she said.

Felicia Griffin and Sean Simpson

Felicia Renaye Griffin and Sean Lorenzo Simpson met in 2014 at an annual conference for statisticians that was held that year in Baltimore. They initially saw each other in a workshop on diversity, but later that evening, they found themselves at the same table at a social event, and a spark between them ignited.

“I was attracted to her ambition and intellect, and also she seemed really humble and genuine and sweet,” Dr. Simpson said. “She’s also assertive. I like that about her.”

Dr. Griffin recalled, “We started talking, and talked and talked and talked. He was dating someone, and so was I. We were attracted to to each other, but had to leave it at that.”

The two exchanged business cards and over the next months had contact sporadically. She was finishing up graduate school in Florida, and he was in Winston-Salem.

The following year, at the very same conference, they met again.

It wasn’t a surprise. Both were on conference committees, and they had exchanged email on matters relevant to the conference, and so they expected to see each other. But for Dr. Griffin, at least, there was some planning ahead to take care of.

“It was kind of like, yeah, you know, let me make sure I’m looking really great because I know I’m going to see him there,” she said.
