Big City Book Club: ‘Happiness Is a Choice You Make’ by John Leland

Big City Book Club with John Leland

John Leland, Times reporter and author, discussed his new book, “Happiness Is a Choice You Make,” based on his 2015 series for the Times that explored the lives New Yorkers 85 and over.

    Ruth, thanks for that. Our separation of generations is an experiment whose consequences we haven’t fully accounted for. Cultures used to turn to their oldest members for wisdom. Now we turn to Google. And Google knows a lot. But it doesn’t know what it’s like to get old, which is the one thing most of us are going to do.

    Last year mom at 80 fell down a flight of stairs. She was terribly hurt, broken wrists, concussion on both sides of her head. She has been in a wheelchair for more than a year and seeming to do poorly. We discovered she wasn’t getting her Alzheimers meds. She has been taking them for the last several months and now is finally coming out of her “coma, I want to say”. She is communicating better and wants to go home, but doesn’t have the strength to walk so she falls out of the wheelchair. I am afraid the Nursing home will give her more Remeron and she will revert to her sleepy self. Yes, we can let Fate take its course but this dilemma is unnerving to me and my three sisters. My brother doesn’t care at all about her. Anyone have suggestions? We think we will just have to go day by day.

    John Leland

    Author

    April 9, 2018, 8:42 PM ET

    That’s a heart-rending situation, Norma. Every family has to make its own decisions about such matters. My own inclination, more and more, is to err on the side of doing what our elders want with their lives. They’ve earned the right to make those decisions, even if we think they’re the wrong ones.
