    California Fires Map: Tracking the Spread

    By MATTHEW BLOCH, JUGAL K. PATEL and ANJALI SINGHVI UPDATED NOV. 21, 2018

    Several fast-moving wildfires are raging through California near the Sierra Nevada foothills and the Los Angeles shoreline. The fires have killed at least 84 people since they began on Nov. 8 and have burned nearly 250,000 acres in the state. The largest of these wildfires, called the Camp Fire, is both the deadliest and the most destructive in California’s modern history.

    The Camp Fire began the morning of Nov. 8 and quickly wiped out the entire town of Paradise. It has spread to more than 150,000 acres, leaving at least 81 people dead and more than 18,000 structures destroyed so far. Firefighters have battled strong, dry winds from the northeast while trying to prevent the fire from spreading, and reported that it was 80 percent contained as of Wednesday morning.

    The Woolsey Fire near Los Angeles began shortly after the Camp Fire, in the afternoon of Nov. 8. It has since covered more than 95,000 acres and destroyed 1,500 structures. On Tuesday, officials said the fire was about 98 percent contained. Fire crews have faced steep terrain, limited access and unpredictable conditions in trying to control the blaze. A body was found in a burned-out home in the Agoura Hills area on Nov. 13, bringing the death toll to three, according to the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department.
