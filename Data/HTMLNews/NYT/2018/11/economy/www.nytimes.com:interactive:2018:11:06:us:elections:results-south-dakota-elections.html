Jan. 28, 2019, 10:38 AM ET

South Dakota Election Results

In recent decades, few states have been less hospitable to Democratic candidates than South Dakota. But in State Senator Billie Sutton, a former professional rodeo competitor, Democrats believe they have found a viable candidate for governor.

Mr. Sutton has run as a centrist who opposes abortion and says that, as a rancher himself, he understands the state’s agriculture-focused economy.

Republicans are hardly conceding the race. Their candidate, Kristi Noem, a farmer and rancher, is well known to South Dakotans as their sole member of the House of Representatives. Ms. Noem, a conservative who prevailed in a fractious Republican primary for governor, has tried to portray Mr. Sutton as a liberal whose priorities are a poor fit for South Dakota.
