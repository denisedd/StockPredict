Utah Election Results

Crimson-red Utah has been a safe state for Republicans, and this held true in the state’s Senate race this year.

For the Senate, a familiar face — the former Republican presidential nominee Mitt Romney — handily beat Jenny Wilson, a Democratic member of the Salt Lake City Council. The incumbent, Orrin Hatch, the longest-serving Republican Senator in the nation’s history, chose not to seek an eighth term. During the campaign, Mr. Romney positioned himself as a leader of a moderate, establishment brand of conservatism at a time when a handful of senators of that ilk were leaving office.

An embattled Republican incumbent, Mia Love, is in a tight race with her Democratic challenger, Ben McAdams. Ms. Love, the lone black Republican woman in Congress, is battling accusations that she raised campaign funds improperly during her primary election; she has acknowledged some errors but characterized the allegations as a politically motivated attack. Mr. McAdams, the mayor of Salt Lake County and a former state senator, has seized on the accusations and held them up as an example of a culture of corruption in Washington that he promises to combat. The race is regarded as a toss-up.
