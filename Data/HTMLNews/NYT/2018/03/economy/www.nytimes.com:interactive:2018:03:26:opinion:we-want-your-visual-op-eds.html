    We Want Your Visual Opinion Pieces

    MARCH 26, 2018

    Calling all visual thinkers: We are now accepting visual op-eds for publication in The New York Times Opinion section in print and online.

    What’s a visual op-ed?

    Op-eds are opinion pieces written by outside contributors and published in The New York Times Opinion section.

    Visual op-eds are reported and researched opinion pieces that use visuals as the main way to convey your argument. These visuals can include graphics, data visualizations, photos, interactives, games, simulations, videos or something else entirely. You can create these visuals, supply them from a source or work with us to make them.

    We encourage you to consult “Tips for Aspiring Op-Ed Writers” before submitting. We accept visual opinion pieces on any topic, but we are prioritizing original visual ideas that cannot be told in any other format.

    Do you have any examples?

    Visual op-eds come in innumerable forms, but here are some examples:

  1. A long read with visual elements that are crucial to understanding the story, like “America’s Wildest Place Is Open for Business”

  2. A chart-based piece where the data is the entire point of the argument, like “Our Broken Economy, in One Simple Chart”

  3. A snappily written argument made through visuals and using our “tap essay” format, like “Automated Vehicles Can’t Save Cities”

  4. An argument made more understandable through video and design, like “What Trump’s Speech Says About His Mental Fitness”

  5. A vision of the future brought to life through 3D renderings, like “Penn Station, Reborn”

  6. A video game with a message, like “The Voter Suppression Trail”

    7. What are the requirements?

    When submitting, please supply:

  7. A story summary. Keep this under 150 words. Focus on your argument and what readers will learn.

  8. A visual summary. Keep this under 150 words. Describe the visuals, including any data you intend to use. You can include sketches of your idea or links to visuals/data you intend to use, but please do not attach any datasets. You must have a clear sense of what the visuals will be and how they will be created.

    9. All submissions must be original and exclusive to The Times. We will not consider articles that have already been published, in any form, in print or online. Unfortunately, because the number of submissions is so large, we have to decline much material of value and interest, and cannot reply to all submissions. If you do not hear from us within three business days, please assume that we will not be able to use your article. You should then feel free to offer it elsewhere.

    How do I submit my visual op-ed?

    Submissions should be sent by email to opinion@nytimes.com with “Visual Op-Ed” in the subject line.
