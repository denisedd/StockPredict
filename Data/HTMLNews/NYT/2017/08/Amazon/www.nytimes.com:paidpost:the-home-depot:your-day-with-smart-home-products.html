    Your Day With Smart Home Products

    The next 24 hours of your life could run more efficiently, cost less and be more secure.

    From the moment you wake up, you’re confronted with a multitude of household details that use up precious time and energy. Picture this instead: Your daily commute is less stressful, with no worry about whether you locked the front door, turned off all the lights or shut off the AC — plus, you’re saving money. This freedom is already possible thanks to Smart Home devices — 26 percent of U.S. households with Wi-Fi have at least one. "There’s a huge demand from people who want more control of their home when they’re not there," says Elizabeth Mathes, Director of Smart Home at The Home Depot. But the number of product options can be daunting. An easy way to start figuring out how to improve security and efficiency in your home is to examine the way you move throughout a full day. Here’s a look.

    5:00 AM

    The Rain Bird Smart Irrigation Wi-Fi Timer stands out from an offline sprinkler because it factors in the local weather. So if it storms overnight, don’t worry about overwatering or having to run out in your pajamas to reset your sprinkler system — the Rain Bird will automatically hold off when the barometer drops. During the fall and winter months, the system calibrates itself to use less water, saving you money. On top of that, if there’s a malfunction in the pipes, the app on your phone alerts you, and it just takes a few taps to shut the system down.

    12:00 AM

    Once you install your Nest Learning Thermostat and begin adjusting the temperature to your preferences, the device gets smarter. Within a few days, it will begin automatically setting itself to your climate-control habits. Imagine sleeping through the night at a comfortable 68 degrees and slowly waking up to a temperate 74 degrees. As Nest tracks your routine, it can also start to conserve energy automatically — like shutting down when you leave for work — and you can track your energy use in the app.

    5:45 AM

    If you’re waking before sunrise or coming home after dark, you can set the Leviton Decora Smart Wi-Fi Universal Dimmer to turn on the lights (individually or as a group) as soon as it gets light or the sun sets. You also won’t have to run around your home turning them all off one by one before you head out or go to sleep at the end of the night — everything can be controlled through your phone app or an intelligent personal assistant, such as Alexa of Amazon’s Echo or OK Google.

    1:00 PM

    Most of us rarely think about our water heater — until it stops working or there’s a crisis. Fortunately, the Rheem EcoNet Water Heaters with Wi-Fi Module Included, has a built-in leak detection system that can send alerts straight to your phone. You can even set it to vacation mode when you’re away.

    5:30 PM

    When you’re unable to be home for urgent situations, like a leaking water heater (see above), the Kevo Touch-to-Open™ smart lock with Kevo Plus lets you unlock your door remotely with your phone. The lock also allows you to monitor who’s coming and going — kids, a tenant or a house cleaner. Just give users their own standard key, Kevo fob or encrypted eKey (to use with a compatible smartphone), and they can all be monitored and reset anytime with the app. Thanks to Bluetooth technology, the lock can also detect if your smartphone is nearby and be opened with a touch of your finger — no more fumbling for keys.

    8:00 PM

    We all know that LED bulbs save money over time compared to a same-wattage incandescent light bulb. Cree Connected Soft White LED bulbs take efficiency one step further, though: They can be programmed to turn on and off automatically, illuminate at preset brightness levels or be instantly shut off using the mobile app from anywhere.

    11:00 PM

    Home fires burn faster than ever now due to architectural styles and synthetic furnishings, so it’s vital to detect the first signs of smoke. The Nest Protect smoke and carbon monoxide alarm can detect both fast- and slow-burning fires. When your Nest detects a problem, it will give you verbal warnings that range from a cautionary “heads up” to more urgent messages accompanied by an alarm. You can also combine your system with the Nest Learning Thermostat, which communicates with the Nest Protect using the internet and Wi-Fi in your home and can shut off forced-air heating systems that use gas, oil or other fossil fuels in a CO emergency. “That’s the beauty of the smart home — when these products start working together and create meaningful solutions,” Mathes says.

    Now you have a sense of how your day could go, make sure you have the basics (a smartphone and strong Wi-Fi), then decide whether you want to solve a few specific problems or install a totally integrated smart-home system. (Even if you start small, you may eventually find yourself wanting to go bigger.) Map out which products work together so you have the option of consolidating with a home automation hub, such as the Wink Hub, down the road. It’s a simple way to connect all your devices to a single control platform so you don’t have to open individual apps, and it can also be activated through your intelligent personal assistant. Experts are predicting the number of smart devices sold worldwide will double by the end of 2017 from last year, so don't be surprised if you end up going big.
