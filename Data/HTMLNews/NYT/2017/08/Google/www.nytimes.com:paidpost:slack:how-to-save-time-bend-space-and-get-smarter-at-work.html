    PAID FOR AND POSTED BY SLACK

    How to Save Time, Bend Space and Get Smarter at Work

    One of the modern office’s great mysteries is the elasticity of time, space and knowledge. An abundance of digital tools makes the clock seem like it’s ticking faster as we gain efficiency. Meanwhile, video conferences and real-time messaging collapse the distance between far-flung colleagues and customers. And knowledge — arguably the most important asset of an organization — is now right at our fingertips, not stashed away in file cabinets.

    But what technology giveth, technology can taketh away. Time, space and knowledge move in the opposite direction when you’re juggling logins, forgetting passwords and getting locked out of all those marvelous apps. Same goes for running a report that incorporates stats from multiple databases and then sharing it via email before holding a presentation — that only happens after scheduling and rescheduling through yet another app. But the messaging platform Slack has helped wrangle all these disparate channels into one place. Slack is not just a home for chats — it’s become an indispensable portal for connected apps, workflows and processes. Here’s how Slack is reshaping the day for office workers around the world and helping bend time, space and knowledge for the better.

    How to Travel Through Time

    Sometimes the most time-consuming tasks are the toughest to automate because they involve many small decisions, each requiring a little bit of judgment — for example, setting up a meeting. Finally found the perfect slot after negotiating different time zones and shifting online calendars? Try again — one VIP now has a conflict! Time is our most valuable asset, so cutting down the hours a typical office worker spends scheduling meetings is imperative.

    This was a familiar story to Jill, a recruiter at a global financial-services firm. Her days used to drag as she wrangled the schedules of busy executives across the U.S. and Europe to carve out time for them to meet with potential hires. Now, all those tiny decisions are made for her and presented in one place — on Slack. With a simple command, integrated apps like Kyber and Meekan Scheduling Assistant use artificial intelligence and natural-language processing to check everyone’s Google Calendar, find available slots and present the information as a Slack message.

    Time, space and knowledge move in the opposite direction when you’re juggling logins, forgetting passwords and getting locked out of all those marvelous apps.

    Jill’s workdays were mired with the drudgery of scheduling and paperwork...

    ...instead of seeking and meeting potential hires.

    Now she’s back to spending most of her days doing her real work — finding talent.

    But Jill has found the time-saving app integrations in Slack go far beyond setting up meetings. Instead of individually following up with interviewers, she can ask the team to vote with emojis. If she needs a more sophisticated polling system, she’ll tap tools such as SimplePoll or Polly. What’s more, she can integrate apps like Trello and JIRA to get quick updates or take actions on major projects, like the rollout of her firm’s new recruiting system. Time flies when Jill actually does the job she loves: recruiting talent.

    Tricks to Transcend Space

    Time isn’t the only thing bent by modern office technology. Consider space, or distance. It’s often easier to work from home or do business from wherever you happen to be, whether it’s a remote office or, better yet, a remote beach. The distance between your company and its employees, business partners and customers can shrink — or grow — based on the apps you use and how you’re using them.

    Nancy oversees a sprawling call center that connects her company’s customers around the world with the people who can fix their issues. The problem is that those workers are also scattered. Trouble tickets by the hundreds are logged in each day — it’s Nancy’s job to make sure not only that the call center is staffed but that trends are identified and escalated to the right people.

    Instead of watching multiple programs for problem alerts and trying to make sure nothing slips through the cracks, Nancy can now monitor everything on Slack. With Slack-integrated customer service apps like ZenDesk, Help Scout and FreshDesk, she can see when tickets are created, modified and resolved in the same window where conversations about issues are taking place. For major and minor problems, everyone involved can be brought together instantly for a video meeting with a single command from Slack. Nancy uses Zoom, but you can choose whichever video app you prefer, such as BlueJeans, Google Hangouts or Slack’s built-in video calls.

    Nancy also uses Slack to manage her team’s vacations with the Zenefits app and sees who’s in and who’s out on any given day with AttendanceBot. And when she literally needs to be in two places at once — say, in an emergency meeting while she’s expecting visitors in the lobby, Envoy makes that possible. An iPad at the reception desk invites visitors to log in, and a Slack notification is sent to Nancy. In the meantime, visitors can print their own security badges so all she needs to do is pick them up when she’s ready.

    Access Knowledge in an Instant

    Knowledge is a company’s most important asset, and networks like the internet have made all sorts of information accessible. That is, if you can find what you need fast enough: You may know the right database, but you’re not sure how to navigate its arcane menus.

    Having to knock on several digital doors a day didn’t sit well with Drew, a marketer whose job depends on finding the right information at the right time. Today, when he’s asked to provide a data-rich report, he relies on Slack integrations to tap a variety of data sources without ever having to leave the interface. The latest update on an opportunity in Salesforce? He just asks his Statsbot app to run a query and the results pop up in Slack in seconds. Ditto for those other silos of data, like Google Analytics, SQL Server and PostgreSQL.

    Sometimes, of course, information isn’t in a digital database, it’s locked in the heads of colleagues — one of whom just took early retirement, while another left for three weeks in India to find himself. That’s why the Guru Slack bot has become one of Drew’s favorites. He can save key bits of information from conversations in Slack and make them searchable for future reference. And to save the links shared on Slack, his team uses Paperbot, which organizes the links so they can be viewed at a glance anytime from anywhere. It’s like catching thoughts in midair.

    Networks like the internet have made all sorts of information accessible — that is, if you can find it in time for it to be useful.

    Slowed down by piles of data from various sources, Drew used to spend ages creating a report for his boss.

    But then he discovered a bot that consolidates key bits of information in Slack to make them searchable for future reference.

    Now, Slack can process his data and present it in a single location...

    ...and Drew’s boss is thinking about giving him a promotion.

    Today, comprehensive communication platforms like Slack have become vital to focus on the jobs we were hired to do. Office software is meant to help us, but it can be overwhelming — the average enterprise now uses more than 1,000 apps to manage everything from entire departments to birthday celebrations. Simplifying and coordinating day-to-day operations is key. And don’t forget the tasks that make everything more fun: Free donuts in the conference room @here?
