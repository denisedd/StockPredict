    You Fix It: Can You Stay Within the World’s Carbon Budget?

    By STUART A. THOMPSON and JESSIA MA AUG. 29, 2017

    Staying within the earth’s carbon budget will be tough — especially since we’ve blown through more than half of it already.

    8 , 100 Gigatons
    The world is projected to emit this much CO2 by 2100, exceeding our carbon budget three times over.

    By Andrew P. Jones, Stuart A. Thompson and Jessia Ma

    We need to stay within our carbon budget.

    To prevent the worst effects of global warming, we have to keep temperatures from increasing by more than 3.6 degrees Fahrenheit (2.0 degrees Celsius) above the preindustrial level — the upper limit agreed to in the 2015 Paris climate accord.

    That means we can’t send more than 2,900 gigatons of CO2 into the atmosphere. This is our carbon budget.

    We’ve already used about 73% of our budget.

    The world has emitted 2,100 gigatons of CO2 since 1870, mostly from developed countries that prospered and polluted from the Industrial Revolution to today.

    The United States played an outsize role here, responsible for about 20% of emissions despite having just 4.4% of the world’s population. Overall, countries in the developed world account for 19% of the world’s population but are responsible for more than half of all emissions to date.

    You did it!

    There are some indications that this kind of success may be possible. Wind and solar, which are growing by double digits in some parts of the world, are zero-carbon energy sources and cost less than coal in many countries. Energy-efficient buildings, motors and appliances are reducing how much energy is needed for economic growth. Many developing countries are bypassing fossil fuels and choosing low-carbon energy.

    If those trends continue, the world might be able to stay within its carbon budget. What's needed most is political will and public support.

    You’re still short of reaching the budget, but that’s expected.

    Staying under the budget requires large emission cuts starting immediately. This is the point climate scientists have been trying to make for some time — that countries must commit to aggressive reductions in carbon emissions to prevent disastrous effects of climate change. Even if all countries met their Paris targets, it still wouldn’t be enough.

    Don’t give up — technological and policy trends suggest we can still make great progress. Try some more aggressive reductions and see whether you can reduce CO2 emissions even more to stay within the budget.
