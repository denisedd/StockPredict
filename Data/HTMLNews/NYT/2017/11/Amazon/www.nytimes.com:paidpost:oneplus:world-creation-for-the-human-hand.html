    PAID FOR AND POSTED BY ONEPLUS

    World Creation for
    the Human Hand

    The world’s most popular free-to-download game is only as successful as the device that houses it.

    There’s nothing quite like an electronic game to transport us from the physical world into fantastical realms. From the very first arcade game — Bertie the Brain, the tick-tack-toe program invented by Josef Kates — in 1950, to the Ataris of the 1970s and ’80s, to the Sega Saturns of the 1990s, to the PlayStations of the 2000s, to the introduction of 3-D and virtual reality options in recent years, our appetite for new, immersive worlds in which to play has only grown.

    China's Favorite
    Mobile Game

    In China, the mobile game du jour is King of Glory, a multiplayer game in which players take up the avatars of swashbuckling heroes and battle other players, accumulating points along the way to unlock special functions and level up. If you’re walking the streets of Beijing or Shanghai and see someone glued to his smartphone tapping away, there’s a good chance he’s one of the 80 million daily players of the game, which has more than 200 million active players latched onto their phones every month. Indeed, smartphones have become portals to wondrous worlds.

    There are many reasons for King of Glory’s success: an easily understandable premise, lush graphics paired with exciting special effects and a highly engaged and supportive gaming community. But the game experience is only as good as the smartphone on which it is played; jerky transitions, poor loading speed or inferior graphics can turn off the user. On the flipside, a powerful machine — such as the smartphones produced by Shenzhen-based OnePlus, a company focusing on creating Android flagship models — can propel a player up the ranks.

    It has an open high-frame rate, which makes for a very stable and seamless playing experience.
    Yi Cai, King of Glory player

    Gamers Discover OnePlus

    “With my OnePlus phone, I’ve hit the top levels playing King of Glory,” says Guo Xuesu, a 31-year-old gamer from China’s Jiang Su province, who is the fifth-ranking King of Glory player in his region. After being constantly frustrated by jittery animation when using other phones, Guo switched to a OnePlus phone after advice from a friend. “I had never heard of OnePlus before,” he confesses, saying that he was looking for a device that could handle live broadcasting — gamers often share their playing experiences live on the internet to the community — while playing a game and listening to music. “When I use my OnePlus phone, the whole playing process is very smooth, and it really feels that I’m flying through the game.”

    The Genesis of OnePlus

    “The OnePlus phone is very powerful because it’s equipped with a top-level processor,” adds another gamer, Yi Cai, from Hebei province, who became a OnePlus user in 2016. “It has an open high-frame rate, which makes for a very stable and seamless playing experience.” Since OnePlus was established in 2013 by its C.E.O. and founder Pete Lau, who has over 20 years of hardware engineering experience, it has released seven models in what many say is an already saturated market.

    “Although the competition was fierce, the existing Android handsets suffered from obvious imperfections,” says Lau. “I thought, why not create the ‘perfect’ Android handset?” The original aim was simple: with the customer at the center of the company’s focus, OnePlus was going to build an intuitive, ergonomic device that delivered superb performance at an affordable price. “A good product speaks for itself. The ethos at OnePlus was, and still is, ‘never settle.’”

    Engineered for Peak Performance

    The latest model, the OnePlus 5T, was released internationally in November. Sporting OnePlus’s signature sleek silhouette, the 5T has its fingerprint sensor moved to the back to make way for a 6.01-inch, edge-to-edge screen with a longer 18:9 aspect ratio, creating a more immersive gaming experience. The navigation buttons have also been incorporated into the touchscreen. “The wider aspect ratio allows for more visual information at the margins, which can be very useful when playing a game like King of Glory,” says Louis Sun, product manager. Meanwhile, facial recognition enables swift unlocking of the phone without the need to type in a password or PIN, and a special “Gaming Do Not Disturb” mode ensures a gaming experience uninterrupted by message notifications.

    When multiple apps are running at the same time, the system is optimized to always give peak performance most efficiently. “We’ve chosen to go with a Snapdragon 835 processor, an 8 GB RAM and a proprietary algorithm system we’ve called UX-Realm that maximizes performance so that the user experience will be most seamless,” adds Sun. The smartphone’s charging mechanism is also designed to ensure that the phone itself doesn’t heat up when users play King of Glory and charge their phone at the same time.

    A good product speaks for itself. The ethos at OnePlus was, and still is, ‘never settle.’
    Pete Lau, C.E.O. and founder of OnePlus

    Just as important as the phone’s capabilities are the actual look and feel of the physical product. “People can tell a quality product just by touching it,” says Sun of the phone’s matte black finish, Bauhaus-inspired exterior and the slim bezel that wraps around the screen. “We’ve designed it in a way that is comfortable to the grip, even after long stretches of time.” The phone also boasts a 16- and 20-megapixel dual camera and retains a feature that many new smartphones are losing — the headphone jack. “Our users play an important role during the creation process and we listen to their feedback,” explains Lau. “They also set a very high threshold for what it means to create a good product.”

    Designed in China for the Global Market

    With a retail price beginning at $499, the OnePlus 5T affords great value at that price point, especially considering that most of its competitors start close to the thousand-dollar mark. “We rely on our official website and e-commerce platforms such as Amazon to retail our products instead of offline channels,” says Lau, adding that the cost savings are passed on to the customer.

    While OnePlus has quickly become the device of choice for an increasing number of avid gamers, the benefits of OnePlus 5T’s incredible specs extend beyond gaming. “Our target is to produce the best-performing phone, and the fact that OnePlus has created the best product for gaming is a byproduct of that aim,” says Xi Zeng, another product manager. He says that because of the smartphone’s processing capabilities, a number of A.I. studios also use OnePlus phones as a creative platform for their work.

    As the C.E.O. and founder of a Chinese brand, Pete Lau aspires for OnePlus to change the world’s opinion that made-in-China products are inferior. “By implementing the highest production processes and adhering to international standards, we have manufactured quality products and built a solid reputation in the global market over the past four years,” he says.

    My wish is that in the future, when people all over the world look to buy an Android phone, they’ll immediately turn to OnePlus.
    Pete Lau, C.E.O. and founder of OnePlus
