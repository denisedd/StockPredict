How Much Watching Time Do You Have This Weekend?

by Margaret Lyons

Nov. 9, 2017

Bridget Everett in “Love You More.”Amazon

No matter how much free time you have this weekend, we have TV recommendations for you. Come back every Friday for new suggestions from our TV critic on what to watch.

This Weekend I Have … a Half-Hour, and I Like to Be Ahead of the Curve

‘Love You More’
When to watch: Now, on Amazon.

Amazon released three new comedy pilots on Friday, each offbeat and mannered. The standout of the batch is this one, written with and starring the cabaret icon Bridget Everett. She plays the boisterous Karen, who works with young adults who have Down syndrome. The show is equal parts earnest and raunchy — and it’s a lot of each.

… an Hour, and I Like Success Stories

Lin-Manuel Miranda, center, in the musical “In the Heights.”Sara Krulwich/The New York Times
‘In the Heights: Chasing Broadway Dreams’
When to watch: Friday at 9 p.m., on PBS (check local listings).

Before there was “a bastard, orphan, son of a whore and a Scotsman,” there was “In the Heights,” Lin-Manuel Miranda’s other beloved, genre-blending, Tony-winning musical. PBS is re-airing this endearing making-of documentary from 2009. It’s even sweeter to watch now, knowing what, er, heights lay ahead for many of the performers and collaborators who went on to work with Mr. Miranda again on “Hamilton.”

… Several Hours, and I Like Unusual Settings

From left, Joel Fry, Tom Rosenthal and Ryan Sampson in “Plebs.”Adriana Yankulova/Rise Films, via Hulu
‘Plebs’
When to watch: Now, on Hulu.

There are many terrifically funny British coming-of-age comedies about groups of young men looking for love and validation. But this is the only one set in ancient Rome. Joel Fry, Tom Rosenthal and Ryan Sampson, star as Stylax, Marcus, and their slave, Grumio, who frequently find themselves on the outside of the most exciting parts of Roman society. The show is filthy, often literally — there’s muck and dirt everywhere — and it’s also affectionate about its historical accuracies.
