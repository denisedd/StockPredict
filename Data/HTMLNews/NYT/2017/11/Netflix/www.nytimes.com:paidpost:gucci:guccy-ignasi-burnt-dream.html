    PAID FOR AND POSTED BY GUCCI

    Loading…

    I t was an artist’s dream come true when Ignasi Monreal was handpicked to create a book of fashion illustrations for Gucci this year. But the fact that he chose one of a winged man (wearing a Gucci suit and backpack) flying toward the sun as the cover image, is no surprise at all.

    Ignasi has always felt an almost magnetic connection with the beauty and power of the sun. His name means “fiery.” And as a young boy in Spain, he became captivated by the story of Icarus, a boy who dared to soar near the sun on wings of feather and wax. “The myth of Icarus is a reminder,” Ignasi says, “Don’t fly too close to the sun.”

    These days, Ignasi says that cautionary tale helps keep him centered in the midst of his own meteoric rise in the fashion world. In 2015, Gucci selected this modern-day Icarus from his inspired posts on social media to collaborate on the digital art project #GucciGram, which invited artists to rework iconic Gucci patterns.

    Today, Ignasi is quickly becoming one of the world’s most sought-after fashion illustrators. He spent an entire summer holed up in his East London apartment, finding inspiration for the 2017 Gucci Gift Catalogue in everything from his couch to the food delivery guy to Netflix — and yes, even Icarus. “If you willingly look at things with a new eye, then you can find inspiration everywhere,” Ignasi says. “Some inspirations are on purpose and some just happen.”
