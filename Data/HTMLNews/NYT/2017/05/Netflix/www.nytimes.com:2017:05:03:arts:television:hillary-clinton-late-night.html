    Hillary Clinton Returns, and Late Night Finds Her ‘Unpresidential’

    Best of Late Night

    By GIOVANNI RUSSONELLO MAY 3, 2017

    Continue reading the main story Share This Page
    Continue reading the main story

    Welcome to Best of Late Night, a rundown that lets you sleep — and lets us get paid to watch comedy. What do you think of it? What else are you interested in? Let us know: thearts@nytimes.com.

    The President of Late Night

    Hillary Clinton appeared at a women’s conference in New York on Tuesday, and late night could think about nothing but the presidency that might have been.

    “Hillary Clinton today said she is very aware of the shortfalls of her campaign that caused her to lose the election. Specifically, Short Falls, Michigan, and Short Falls, Wisconsin.” — SETH MEYERS

    For some hosts, Mrs. Clinton’s appearance threw President Trump’s tenure into stark relief.

    “So she made mistakes, publicly recognized them, and owned up to her shortcomings. No wonder she lost. That’s totally unpresidential.” — STEPHEN COLBERT

    “Wow. I mean, no ‘tremendouses’? No hand gestures? Not one description of chocolate cake? I didn’t realize how much I’d missed hearing sentences with a beginning, middle and end. You can really tell when Hillary’s speaking, she really knows who Frederick Douglass is.” — TREVOR NOAH

    Stephen Colbert Salutes Jimmy Kimmel...

    On Tuesday’s “Late Show,” Stephen Colbert gave Jimmy Kimmel a shout out for his moving monologue on Monday night about his newborn son. Mr. Colbert encouraged viewers to seek out the video if they hadn’t already.

    “Please go watch it. It’s an absolutely beautiful, heartfelt story. And it’s a call for all people to have access to the same quality health care, because it’s a basic human need and an aspect of our dignity as citizens of the United States.” — STEPHEN COLBERT

    ...And Will Arnett Subs for Him

    Jimmy Kimmel is taking the week off to be with his newborn son, but “Live!” isn’t reverting to reruns. The show will welcome a different guest host each night for at least the rest of the week.

    The comedian Will Arnett, a star of “Arrested Development,” filled in for Mr. Kimmel on Tuesday. Clearly, Mr. Kimmel’s widely praised show on Monday was a hard act to follow — so Mr. Arnett managed everyone’s expectations.

    “Just think of me as your depressed middle-aged substitute teacher who you definitely saw cry in his car before coming in.” — WILL ARNETT

    “I gotta say I’m not surprised Jimmy asked me to do this. When you want someone to fill in for you but you don’t want them to be too good, I’m your guy.” — WILL ARNETT

    The Punchiest Punchlines (Unicycle Poetry Edition)

    “There is something a little bit funny about a labor protest in Portland, the hipster capital of the world. You know, it’s like, ‘We’re here for workers’ rights!’ ‘Well, what’s your job, man?’ ‘Oh, I write unicycle poetry.’” — TREVOR NOAH, referring to May Day protests in Oregon that turned chaotic.

    “Analysts are saying that Donald Trump has not read his new health care plan, and in fact he has actually spent no time getting familiar with it. Yeah, the first hint was that he called it Melania.” — CONAN O’BRIEN

    “President Trump had an official call with Vladimir Putin today. I hear the call was tense — but let’s face it, that’s how performance reviews usually go.” — STEPHEN COLBERT

    The Bits Worth Watching

    “This guy will steal your wife during your honeymoon trip, and then write an article about it for Rolling Stone that you will read — and enjoy.” That guy’s a real dog. No, a real dog.

    Is that how it went?

    What We’re Excited About on Wednesday Night

    James Corden likes to set up group interviews with his “Late Late Show” guests. And sometimes the chemistry isn’t there. But the Times Late-Night Comedy Committee will be excited to see Jane Fonda and Lily Tomlin — co-stars in the Netflix series “Grace and Frankie” — appearing on the show together on Wednesday.
