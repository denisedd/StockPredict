    How Accurate Are 2017’s Hit Songs?

    Does Ed Sheeran know his science? Is strawberry champagne on ice a thing? Read on as we fact check your favorite artists from the past year.

    Shape of You

    Ed Sheeran

    The most-streamed song of all time.

    “The club isn’t the best place to find a lover / So the bar is where I go”

    If Mr. Sheeran truly wants to find a lover, he might want to look elsewhere. A 2014 report found that only about 20% of couples meet in bars or restaurants, while around 30% meet during their daily lives. Approximately 70% of same-sex couples, however, meet online versus 30% of heterosexual couples. But Mr. Sheeran is on the right track by being proactive and getting out there — around 43% of relationships begin with self-introductions.

    SOURCE: Psychology Today

    “I’m in love with the shape of you / We push and pull like a magnet do”

    Magnetism is the result of two electrical currents interacting, which creates the fields and poles within a magnet: i.e., the “push-pull.” That said, there are two types of magnets: those that occur naturally, and electromagnets, which are created by applying an electrical current to a conductive metal such as iron. But whichever type of magnet Mr. Sheeran is referring to, he knows his science — both have poles that attract and repel.

    SOURCE: Encyclopaedia Brittanica

    “And last night you were in my room / And now my bed sheets smell like you”

    Intercourse burns about 100 calories per session, so perspiration is a natural outcome. Perspiration, or sweat, contains compounds that can cause bacteria to grow, increasing the chances that body odors will cling to fabric — including clothing or bed linens — into the next day. Perfume, lotions and other products can also leave residual scents that are likely to evoke the memory of the person who used them.

    SOURCES: Public Library of Science, Elsevier.com, Howstuffworks.com

    Malibu

    Miley Cyrus

    Streamed over 250 million times this year.

    “ ‘Cause now I’m as free as birds, catching the wind”

    Most birds are known for their ability to catch the wind — but is it a sign of freedom? Scientists now understand that what looks like effortless gliding actually depends on a bird’s ability to carefully calibrate wind velocity, torque and temperature in order to soar. That said, once a bird hones this ability, it can help it travel thousands of miles with little effort — a freedom of sorts.

    SOURCES: University of California, San Diego, Aerospaceweb

    “I never went boatin’ / I don’t know how they get floatin’ ”

    Ms. Cyrus has, in fact, been on a boat, and there is photographic evidence to prove it. Still, it remains possible that she hasn’t enjoyed actual sailing and may not fully grasp the physics behind flotation. Simply put, an object will float when it weighs the same as the amount of water it displaces. That’s why a small rock will sink, pulled down by the force of gravity, but a much larger boat can float.

    SOURCES: Scientific American, JustJaredJr

    “And it’ll be us, just for a while / Do we even exist?”

    With prominent technology leaders suggesting that we’re constructs created by a distant future A.I., one could argue that our existence isn’t “real” per se. For example: If one concedes that humans will someday create simulations of the world as realistic as this one, and those simulations can create other simulations, then the chance that our own reality is the original reality becomes infinitesimally small. Ms. Cyrus, it seems, is right to question the nature of our reality.

    SOURCE: Scientific American

    Body Like a Backroad

    Sam Hunt

    The most-streamed country song of 2017.

    “Ain’t no curves like hers on them downtown streets”

    Many towns in the U.S. were built on a rectilinear grid plan. In fact, Mr. Hunt’s home of Cedartown, Ga. — his possible point of reference — has few curved roads in its historic district. Back roads, by contrast, exist in rural areas and are known for their winding quality and scenic views. Unlike the national and state highways, which require construction, back roads tend to follow natural footpaths, riverbeds and the like.

    SOURCE: Google Maps

    “Doin’ 15 in a 30, I ain’t in no hurry / I’ma take it slow just as fast as I can”

    Driving 15 m.p.h. over the speed limit is listed as a “serious offense” by the DMV in Georgia, Mr. Hunt’s home state — but driving slowly in a passing lane can be even more dangerous. If you’re lagging in the left lane, other cars may be forced to pass you on the right, which can cause accidents. Also, driving slowly on city or residential roads can be hazardous if it forces other cars going at normal speeds to brake suddenly.

    SOURCES: DMV.org, Esurance.com, Georgia Drivers Manual

    “Got hips like honey, so thick and so sweet”

    Though as sweet as sugar, honey isn’t always thick. When first harvested from the hive, honey is in liquid form and its viscosity depends on many factors, such as flower type, temperature and environment. That said, honey does crystallize over time. Creamed honey is lightly processed to control the crystallization process and give it the consistency of peanut butter. Ergo: Older honey is often thicker.

    SOURCES: Scienceline.org, BeeSource

    That’s What I Like

    Bruno Mars

    Streamed over 575 million times this year.

    “Cool jewelry shining so bright / Strawberry champagne on ice”

    While not an official type of Champagne — which is the designation for the effervescent wine from the Champagne region of France — a strawberry champagne cocktail, possibly akin to a French 75, can be made with muddled fruit, a sparkling wine (like a cava) and a liquor of choice. Certain types of bubbly do possess a rosy hue reminiscent of strawberries, but it would not be advisable to serve these over ice.

    SOURCES: Charlotte Magazine, Bon Appétit

    “Sex by the fire at night / Silk sheets and diamonds all white”

    If you’re going to make love by a fire, sheets made from pure silk — not a polyester-based satin “silk” — are a safe bet. Most fabrics are flammable, but some are more combustible than others. Natural, untreated cotton and silk may ignite from a stray spark, but would likely burn more slowly than, say, a natural-synthetic blend. Either way, keeping a safe distance from a fireplace or wood stove is wise.

    SOURCES: Phoenix.gov, Sewwhatinc.com

    “I will never make a promise that I can’t keep / I promise that your smile ain’t gon’ never leave”

    As well-intentioned as Mr. Mars may be, approximately 60% of adults lie in conversations lasting more than just 10 minutes. And typically the infatuation stage isn’t built to last, either — indeed, only about 18% of couples report being intensely in love after a decade. That said, it’s heartening to know that even if their smiles come and go, many couples do forge enduring bonds, with approximately 30% saying they’re still in love after 20 years.

    SOURCES: Psychology Today, Journal of Social and Personal Relationships
