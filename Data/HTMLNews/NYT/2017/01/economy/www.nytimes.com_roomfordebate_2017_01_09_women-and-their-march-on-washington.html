    Women and Their March on Washington

    Introduction

    High school students in Boise, Idaho, helping to organize a march there in conjunction with the one planned for Washington. Katherine Jones/Idaho Statesman, via Associated Press

    The Women’s March on Washington is the first national demonstration in response to the election of Donald J. Trump. But already it has been criticized as a feel-good initiative, lacking focus and inclusiveness.

    Is this march truly a useful endeavor? Is there a better way for women to raise their concerns about a Trump administration?

    This is part of the Issues for Trump and America series.
