    From Their Pens to Donald Trump’s Ear

    Introduction

    The U.S. Capitol is hung with flags for the Inauguration of President-elect Donald J. Trump on Jan. 20. Aaron P. Bernstein/Getty Images

    We asked speechwriters for the last eight presidents to offer words that they would like to hear from President-elect Donald J. Trump in his Inaugural Address on Jan. 20. Here are their suggestions.

    Read the Discussion »

    Debaters

    • “Four Score … Speaking of Scores, I’ll Be Settling Some.”

      Jerome Doolittle, speechwriter for President Jimmy Carter

    • Acknowledge What’s Wrong Before Making Things Right

      Clark Judge, speechwriter for President Ronald Reagan

    • Start With a Prayer

      Mary Kate Cary, speechwriter for President George H.W. Bush

    • “A Nu and Brite Future for America”

      Noam Neusner, speechwriter for President George W. Bush

    • The Celebrity Apprentice President

      J. Terry Edmonds, speechwriter for President Bill Clinton

    • The Line That Could Really Make the Speech

      David Litt, speechwriter for President Barack Obama

    • You’re a Builder. Build.

      Patrick Butler, speechwriter for President Gerald R. Ford

    • Stand With the Police

      Ben Stein, speechwriter for President Richard M. Nixon
