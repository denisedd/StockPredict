    Amazon Is Trying to Do (and Sell) Everything

    By KARL RUSSELL and ASHWIN SESHAGIRI JUNE 16, 2017

    The company’s $13.4 billion deal for Whole Foods is the latest signal of Amazon’s ambitions to have a hold on nearly every facet our lives — like the computer servers that power our favorite websites and the food we eat. Related Article

    Online Retail

    Amazon has a variety of sites, namely Amazon.com, Zappos as well as smaller ones, like Fabric, which focuses on quilting and sewing materials, and ShopBop, which sells fashion and clothing.

    Its cloud computing business, Amazon Web Services, hauled in $12.2 billion in revenue last year from customers including Netflix and the C.I.A.

    Streaming Video

    Amazon Prime Video, which features a combination of new movies, TV shows and original programming.

    Competes with:

    Production Studio

    Created in 2010, Amazon Studios has produced award-winning movies and shows, including “Manchester by the Sea” and “Transparent.”

    Competes with:

    Large-Scale Delivery

    Its logistics business includes warehouses, an army of workers and even planes. It is also testing drones for delivery to homes.

    Competes with:

    Food Delivery

    Available to Prime members, Amazon Restaurants promises to deliver meals from restaurants to customers in one hour or less.

    Mobile Devices
